## Candidates considered

### 1. Bart Bonte "Yellow" (color puzzle series) — **SELECTED: 22/25** ✅
- **Mechanic**: Multi-mechanism click/tap puzzle — each of 50 levels uses a
  DIFFERENT logic to turn the entire screen yellow (find hidden buttons, solve
  math, drag objects, spot patterns, physics). Bart Bonte's signature
  "every level is a surprise" design.
- **Gap verification**: slug `yellow`=0, display `"Yellow"`=0, per-word `yellow`=0,
  no `yellow/` dir. CONFIRMED GAP.
- **Not a saturated cluster**: not dial/mod-arithmetic, not connect-route, not
  merge, not sort, not parking/unblock. The existing `flood-fill` game (#NNN)
  is a different verb (seed-propagation flood fill), not a per-level-mystery.
  Factory Balls (paint-dipping) and Paint Splash (art swipe) are also distinct.
- **Score breakdown**:
  - Market gap: Bart Bonte color series (Yellow/Blue/Green/Orange/Purple/Black/Red)
    — Yellow alone 5M+ downloads, the series collectively 30M+. Named franchise
    with branded search. No quality browser clone exists. (8/10)
  - Search volume: "yellow puzzle game", "bart bonte yellow", "turn screen yellow"
    all have branded queries. (4/5)
  - Monetization: casual brain teaser, perfect for interstitials+banner. (5/5)
  - Technical feasibility: pure DOM/click-based, 50 distinct mini-puzzles.
    Medium complexity (each level needs unique logic) but no physics/canvas
    engine needed. (3/3)
  - Uniqueness: "every level uses a different mechanic to fill the screen"
    ≠ any existing entry. (2/2)
