How to Play Camshaft Timing

Camshaft Timing is a mechanical phase-alignment puzzle. Each level shows a row of camshafts connected by a timing belt, with valves above each cam. The goal: at a target crank angle (the gold marker at the top), every valve must be OPEN — meaning its cam's lobe points up at that exact angle. Each cam has a fixed lobe pattern (a binary sequence over the crank cycle). You rotate "sub-chains" of coupled cams using the ◀ ▶ buttons in the phase-stepper bar — rotating one cam in a chain shifts them all together because they're belt-coupled. Idler gears (marked "IDL") split the chain into independent sub-chains you can rotate separately. Win when all valves are aligned at the gold target angle. Fewer moves earn more stars (par shown in the HUD).

Tips & Strategy

Watch the Aligned counter in the HUD — it updates live as you rotate, showing how many valves are already correct. Each sub-chain has its own ◀ ▶ control and phase value (φ); turning a sub-chain shifts all its cams by the same amount. When a sub-chain's box turns green, all its cams are aligned — leave it alone and work on the others. In tiers with idlers, treat each independent sub-chain as a separate puzzle: solve them one at a time. The keyboard also works (arrow keys, or number keys 1–6 to target a specific sub-chain).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this game free?

Yes! Camshaft Timing is completely free to play in your browser — no download or sign-up required.

Can I play on mobile?

Yes — tap the ◀ ▶ buttons under each cam group. The interface is fully touch-friendly.

How many levels?

30 levels across 6 tiers (T1–T6). Tiers 1–2 use a single belt with no idlers; Tier 3+ introduces idler gears that split the belt into independent sub-chains; later tiers add more cams and longer crank cycles.

What is an idler gear?

An idler gear sits between two cams and decouples them — the cams on each side of an idler form separate sub-chains that rotate independently, giving you finer control over individual phases.
