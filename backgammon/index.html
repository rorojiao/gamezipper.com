How to Play

1. The Board

Backgammon is played on a board with 24 narrow triangles called "points". The points are numbered 1-24. White moves from point 24 toward point 1 (home), Black moves from point 1 toward point 24 (home).

2. Rolling & Moving

Each turn you roll two dice and move checkers forward by the numbers shown. You can split the dice between two checkers or use both on one. Doubles give you four moves instead of two.

3. Hitting & The Bar

A point with a single checker is a "blot". If an opponent lands on your blot, your checker is sent to the bar and must re-enter the board before other moves can be made.

4. Bearing Off

Once all your checkers are in your home board (points 1-6 for White, 19-24 for Black), you can start bearing them off. Roll the exact number or higher to remove checkers. First to bear off all 15 wins!
