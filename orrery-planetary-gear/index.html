How to Play Orrery Planetary Gear

Orrery Planetary Gear is an engineering puzzle based on epicyclic (planetary) gearsets. The mechanism has three concentric components: the Sun gear (orange, center), the Planet-Carrier (cyan, middle, holding planet gears), and the Ring gear (pink, outer). Each component carries a marker dot. Your goal is to rotate the dials so that all three markers end up on the same radial line — meaning the gearset is perfectly aligned.

Each dial rotates all three components simultaneously by a fixed triple of tooth-ratio increments (shown as S±n R±n C±n, where n is in units of the current N positions). The trick is that each dial shifts all three by different amounts, so you must find the right combination of dials to co-align them. The system uses modular arithmetic (mod N), where N goes from 6 to 12 across levels.

Controls: Tap any dial to rotate all three gearset components by its triple. Use ↶ Undo to reverse, ⟲ Reset to restart, ✓ Align? to check, and ▦ Levels for level select. On desktop: number keys (1–9) for dials, Z to undo, R to reset, Enter/Space to check.

Tips & Strategy

  • Watch for the green glow: When all three markers align, a green circle appears around the gearset. This is your visual win confirmation.
  • Think in differences: Co-alignment means S=R=C mod N. Focus on which dial combinations reduce the angular differences between the three markers to zero.
  • Stars reward efficiency: Three stars for the optimal solution, two stars within opt+2, one star otherwise. Use Undo to explore and find shorter paths.
  • Difficulty scales with dials and positions: Levels 1–6 have 2 dials (N=6), 7–12 have 3 dials (N=8), 13–18 have 3 dials (N=10), 19–24 have 4 dials (N=12), and 25–30 have 5 dials (N=12).
