# Hex Haven — Competitive Benchmark

## Selected Concept
A Dorfromantik-style hex tile landscape builder. Player places hexagonal biome tiles onto a hex grid, matching edges to grow forests, fields, waterways, towns, and railways. Quest objectives drive scoring.

## Top 3 Direct Competitors

### 1. Dorfromantik (Toukana Interactive, 2022)
- **Reviews**: 50,000+ Steam, "98% Overwhelmingly Positive"
- **Awards**: Gamescom 2022 Best Casual, BAFTA nominee
- **Levels**: Endless Classic mode (limited tiles per session), Creative sandbox, Hard mode, Monthly Challenge
- **Mechanics**:
  - Hex tile placement, each tile has 6 edge segments
  - Edge types: Forest, Field, Water, Town, Railway
  - Tiles can be single-biome OR multi-biome (split between edges)
  - Quests: "Forest with N+ tiles", "Longest river", "Town size N"
  - Completing quests = bonus tiles + score
  - Game ends when tile queue runs out
- **Scoring**: 1 pt per matching edge; bonus for quests; multiplier for long chains
- **Visual**: Watercolor, hand-painted, soft pastels, isometric slight angle
- **Music**: Ambient folk, soft piano, acoustic guitar

### 2. Islanders (GrizzlyGames, 2019)
- **Reviews**: 30,000+ Steam, "97% Positive"
- **Mechanics**:
  - Pack-based: get a pack of 5-7 buildings, place each for points
  - Score based on adjacency (e.g., Mansion next to water = +score, next to industry = penalty)
  - Reach score threshold → next pack; fail → game over
- **Scoring**: Numeric thresholds per pack, scaling difficulty
- **Visual**: Low-poly, miniature diorama
- **Music**: Relaxing ambient

### 3. Pan'orama / Tiledom (mobile clones, 2023)
- **Downloads**: millions each
- **Mechanics**: Simplified Dorfromantik — hex tiles, edge match, score, ads between packs
- **Free-to-play**: in-app purchases for tile packs, daily challenges

## Hex Haven Specific Design

### Tile Biomes (6 types, plus hybrids)
1. **Forest** (deep green) — chains grow into "woods"
2. **Field/Meadow** (warm yellow-green) — chains grow into "plains"
3. **Water** (blue) — chains become "rivers" and "lakes"
4. **Town** (terracotta rooftops) — chains become "villages"
5. **Railway** (steel gray) — chains become "rail lines"
6. **Mountain** (purple-gray peaks) — chains become "ranges"

Hybrid tiles have edge splits (e.g., forest/water edge, town/field).

### Level Structure (20 levels)
- **Tutorial 1-3**: Single biome matching, small grid (3-tile radius)
- **Beginner 4-7**: Two biomes, medium grid (4-radius)
- **Intermediate 8-12**: Three biomes + quests, larger grid (5-radius)
- **Advanced 13-17**: All biomes + hybrid tiles + harder quests
- **Expert 18-20**: Large grids + complex quests + tile scarcity

### Quest Types
- "Grow a forest of N tiles"
- "Build a town of N tiles"
- "Connect water tiles into a chain of N"
- "Create a railway of length N"
- "Reach a score of N"
- "Place N tiles of type X"

### Scoring Formula
- Base: +1 per matching adjacent edge
- Chain bonus: contiguous biome group of N tiles = +N² / 10 bonus
- Quest completion: +50 per quest
- Tier star: 1★ at 100pts, 2★ at 250pts, 3★ at 500pts

## Visual Style
- Dark cozy palette (GameZipper dark) but with warm accent tones
- Each biome: gradient fill + subtle texture
- Hex tiles with rounded edges, soft drop shadow
- Particle effect on tile placement (small bloom)
- Confetti burst on quest completion
- Smooth drop-in animation (200ms ease-out)
- Day/night ambient shift in background tint
- Title "Hex Haven" with text-shadow glow, NO text-stroke
