How to Play Anemometer Wind Map

Anemometer Wind Map is a meteorological logic puzzle where you rotate cup anemometers so that each one's reading matches its target wind direction (one of 8 compass directions: N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW). The challenge is that each anemometer's reading is influenced by wind coupling from the previous one — specifically, reading = (your rotation + previous anemometer's rotation) mod 8, with the first one coupled to the external wind direction shown at the top.

Controls: Tap any cup anemometer (or click on desktop) to rotate it clockwise by 45° (one step). Each tap updates that anemometer's setting, which cascades to affect the reading of the next one. A green reading arrow means it matches the yellow target; white means it doesn't. Use ◀ Prev / Next ▶ to navigate, ↻ Reset to restart a level, and 🔊 to mute sound.

Tips & Strategy

  • Work left to right: Since each anemometer's reading depends on the one before it, adjust the leftmost anemometer first, then work your way right — each fix may ripple downstream.
  • Watch the external wind: The wind vane at the top shows the external wind direction that feeds into the first anemometer. This is fixed per level — account for it when solving.
  • Use the "Read/Want" labels: Below each anemometer, the current reading and desired target are displayed. Match all of them to win.
  • More anemometers = harder: Levels 1–10 have 3, levels 11–20 have 4, levels 21–25 have 5, and levels 26–30 have 6 anemometers — more coupling chains to manage.
