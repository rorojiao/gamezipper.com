Why Tasukuea?

Tasukuea (タスクエア, “find squares”) is a Nikoli shading puzzle. The challenge is to discover where the hidden squares are — each clue tells you how many black cells surround it, but not which ones. The square-shape constraint and no-adjacency rule make every puzzle a tight logical knot.

How to Play Tasukuea

Setup. The grid is N×N. Some cells contain a number, a ? mark, or are blank.

Goal. Blacken cells to satisfy all constraints simultaneously.

Rule 1 — Squares. Black cells must form square areas (1×1, 2×2, 3×3, etc.).

Rule 2 — No adjacency. Different squares cannot be orthogonally adjacent (they can touch diagonally).

Rule 3 — White cells. Cells with numbers, “?” marks, or blanks you choose to leave white must remain white.

Rule 4 — Number clues. A numbered cell tells you exactly how many black cells are orthogonally adjacent to it (up/down/left/right).

Rule 5 — ? cells. A ? cell must have at least one adjacent black cell.

Rule 6 — Connectivity. All white cells must form one orthogonally connected area.

Strategy. Start by deducing cells with 0 adjacent blacks — all 4 neighbors must be white. High-count clues constrain many cells. The square-shape rule and no-adjacency rule then narrow the rest.

• Shade mode: tap a blank cell to mark it black; tap again to clear.
• Erase mode: tap a black cell to clear it.
• Check: validates your current state. Errors are highlighted.
• Hint: reveals one correct black cell. You have 3 per level.
• Clear: removes all your shading.
