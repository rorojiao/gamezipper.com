How to Play

Goal

Divide the grid into regions so that each region is 180-degree rotationally symmetric around its galaxy dot, and every region contains exactly one dot.

Controls

Click or tap on the edges between cells to toggle walls on or off. A wall separates two cells into different regions. Remove walls to connect cells into the same region.

Rules

1. Every cell must belong to exactly one region.
2. Every region must contain exactly one dot (galaxy center).
3. Every region must be 180-degree rotationally symmetric around its dot.

Tips

Start from the dots and work outward. Each cell you add to a region must have a partner cell on the opposite side of the dot. Use the Check button to see incorrect walls highlighted in red. Use Hint to automatically fix one wall.

How to Play

  • Divide the grid into regions, each centered on a circle.
  • Every region must be 180° rotationally symmetric around its circle.
  • This means every cell in the region has a mirror partner on the opposite side of the circle.
  • Regions must not overlap and must cover the entire grid.
  • Click and drag to draw boundaries between regions.
  • Some circles are at cell centers, others at cell corners (intersections).

Tips & Tricks

  • Start with circles near edges: A circle close to the border has limited symmetry options — its region is often forced.
  • Draw mirror pairs: For every cell you assign to a region, immediately mark its rotational partner.
  • Check symmetry constantly: After each placement, verify the region still has 180° rotational symmetry around its circle.
  • Corner circles differ: When a circle sits at a grid intersection (corner of 4 cells), the region includes quarter-cells from all four surrounding cells.
  • Look for isolated cells: Cells surrounded by other regions must belong to the nearest available circle.
