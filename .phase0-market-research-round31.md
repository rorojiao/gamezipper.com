### Selected: Quoridor (slug: `quoridor`)

**Mechanic**: Two-player abstract strategy on a 9×9 board. Each player has one
pawn starting on opposite edges. On your turn, EITHER (a) move your pawn one
square orthogonally (forward/sideways, not back), OR (b) place one of your 10
walls spanning two cells between board squares. Wall constraints:
1. Cannot fully enclose either player (path-preservation rule).
2. Limited to 10 walls per player.
3. Walls cannot overlap existing walls.

**Goal**: First pawn to reach the opponent's home row wins. The dynamic wall
placement creates a maze-building race — distinct from static-maze (labyrinth,
maze-runner, maze-paint) and from block-slide (parking-jam, unblock-me).

**Gap Score (25-pt rubric)**:
- Market gap (≥5M reach, no browser clone): 9/10 — Spiel des Jahres 1997
  nominee, Games Magazine "Best Abstract" winner, 7M+ copies sold worldwide,
  in BGG Top-1000 for 25+ years. Multiple mobile apps (9game, gardenpath GH
  project). No quality full-featured browser clone with AI + path-preservation.
- Search volume: 4/5 — Branded search ("quoridor online", "quoridor play"),
  recognized name in board-game community, steady traffic from BGG.
- Monetization potential: 4/5 — PvP+AI sessions 10-20 min, perfect for
  interstitials between rounds + banner. High replay via 3 AI difficulty levels.
- Technical feasibility: 3/3 — Pure DOM/canvas, BFS pathfinding for the
  no-enclosure check + AI evaluation. No physics. AI is minimax-depth-2 with
  wall-distance + path-length heuristics.
- Uniqueness: 2/2 — Mechanic is distinct from:
  - labyrinth/maze-runner/maze-paint (PRE-BUILT static maze; Quoridor builds
    walls DURING play, dynamically reshaping the maze)
  - parking-jam/unblock-me (slide pieces in pre-set jam; Quoridor places walls)
  - chess/checkers/go/shogi/xiangqi (capture-based, not race-to-goal)
  - dominosa/pentomino (placement, but no race + no path preservation)

**Total: 22/25** ✅ EXCEEDS 18/25 threshold

### Decision
**Build Quoridor** as game #530. Single-file HTML5 with:
- 9×9 board, 2 pawns (player=blue, AI=red)
- 10 walls each (placed between squares, spanning 2 cells)
- BFS path-preservation check (cannot fully block either player)
- Move rules: pawn 1 square orthogonally; can jump over adjacent opponent
  pawn if square behind is empty
- Wall placement: between two adjacent squares, must be horizontal or vertical,
  cannot overlap, cannot disconnect either player from their goal row
- AI: 3 difficulty levels (Easy=greedy path-shortening, Medium=minimax-2 with
  wall-distance eval, Hard=minimax-3 with path-differential eval)
- Win: first pawn to reach opponent's home row
- 30 "puzzle scenarios" across 5 tiers (preset board states to find winning
  wall move) + free-play vs AI mode
- Star ratings based on move count efficiency / win margin
