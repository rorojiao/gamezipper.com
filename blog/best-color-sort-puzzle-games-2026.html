What Is a Color Sort Puzzle?

A color sort puzzle is a logic game where you rearrange mixed-up colored items — balls, liquids, nuts, sand, or tiles — so that each container holds only one color. You move items one at a time, and you can only place an item onto a matching color or into an empty space. The puzzle is solvable, but only if you find the right sequence of moves.

The genre traces back to the classic Ball Sort Puzzle that went viral on mobile in 2020, then exploded across browser game portals through 2024-2026. By 2026, the category has splintered into several distinct sub-genres, each with its own mechanics and fanbase:

  • 🔴 Ball Sort — pour colored balls between tubes (the original)
  • 💧 Water Sort — pour colored liquids between flasks (the relaxing variant)
  • 🔩 Nut Sort — unscrew and re-stack colored nuts and bolts (the mechanical variant)
  • 🏖️ Sand Sort — layer colored sand in glass containers (the visual variant)
  • 📦 Goods Sort — sort packaged items by type on a shelf (the match-3 hybrid)

Water Sort (also called Liquid Sort) is the most relaxing variant in the color sort family. Instead of solid balls, you pour colored liquids between glass flasks. The visual of liquids splashing and settling is deeply satisfying, and the puzzle logic is identical to ball sort — but the liquid aesthetic makes it feel calmer and more meditative.

Nut Sort is the fastest-growing color sort sub-genre in 2026. Instead of pouring liquids or balls, you unscrew colored nuts from threaded bolts and re-stack them in the correct order. The mechanical, tactile feel of unscrewing adds a satisfying layer of interaction that pure liquid and ball variants can't match.

Color Sort Strategy: 5 Tips to Solve Any Level

Whether you're playing ball sort, water sort, or nut sort, the same core strategies apply. Master these five principles and you'll be able to solve any color sort level without restarting:

  1. Always keep one empty container. The single most important rule. Without an empty tube, flask, or bolt, you have no buffer space and will get stuck. Protect your empties like gold.
  2. Focus on one color at a time. Don't try to sort everything simultaneously. Pick the color with the most items visible on top and consolidate it fully before moving to the next.
  3. Work from the top down. Look at what's on top of each container and plan backwards. The items buried at the bottom are the ones you need to dig out — figure out where they'll go before you start moving.
  4. Use undo liberally. The undo button is not cheating — it's a planning tool. Try a move, see if it leads anywhere, and undo if it doesn't. Every GameZipper sort game has unlimited undos.
  5. Count your moves before committing. Before making a sequence of pours, mentally trace through 3-4 steps ahead. If you can't see a clear path to the next consolidation, you're probably moving the wrong item.
