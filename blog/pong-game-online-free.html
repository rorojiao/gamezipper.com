How to Play Pong

The rules are beautifully simple:

  • Move your paddle up and down to hit the ball back to your opponent.
  • Don't miss — if the ball passes your paddle, your opponent scores a point.
  • First to 11 points wins (or whatever score you set).
  • Use angles — where the ball hits your paddle affects its return angle.

The History of Pong

Pong was created by Allan Alcorn for Atari in 1972. It was originally a training exercise but became so addictive that the prototype broke down from too many quarters being stuffed into it. Pong went on to spawn the arcade industry, home consoles, and eventually the modern gaming world we know today. The simplicity of two paddles and a ball remains one of the most elegant game designs ever created.

Pong Tips and Strategy

  1. Stay centered — keep your paddle near the middle of the screen so you can react to any shot.
  2. Use the edges — hitting the ball with the edge of your paddle creates sharper angles that are harder to return.
  3. Watch the ball's speed — in many versions, the ball speeds up over time. Stay ready!
  4. Don't over-correct — small, smooth movements beat frantic jerking. Keep your cool.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I play Pong on my phone?

Yes! Browser-based Pong games work on iPhone, Android, and any mobile browser. Touch controls let you drag your paddle up and down.

Is Pong the first video game ever?

Pong (1972) is one of the earliest arcade video games and the first commercially successful one, but not the very first. It popularized the video game industry.

How do you win at Pong?

The key is anticipation — watch the ball's angle and position your paddle early. Use the edges of your paddle to change the ball's angle and create shots your opponent can't reach.

Can two players play Pong together?

Originally Pong was a two-player game. Many browser versions support multiplayer with shared keyboard (W/S for player 1, Up/Down for player 2).
