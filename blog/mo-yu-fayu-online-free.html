SlackOff Defense Tips & Tricks

1. Home row is your best friend

Keep your fingers on ASDF and JKL; — the home row. This gives you the fastest access to the most common letters. When you need to type quickly, starting from home row saves precious milliseconds.

2. Read ahead

Don't try to type one letter at a time. Glance at the incoming text and read the whole word or phrase first. Your brain can process words faster than your fingers can type individual letters.

3. Prioritize threats

Some incoming tasks are longer than others. When multiple tasks are coming, deal with the longer ones first — they'll take more time and become dangerous if left unattended.

4. Use muscle memory

SlackOff Defense trains your fingers to recognize common office phrases. Once you've played a few rounds, you'll start anticipating words before you even consciously read them. This is the key to high scores.

5. Take breaks between rounds

If you're practicing to improve your real-world typing speed, play in short bursts rather than marathon sessions. Short, focused practice is more effective than grinding.
