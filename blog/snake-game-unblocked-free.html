How to Play Snake

Snake is one of the simplest yet most addictive games ever created:

  • Desktop: Use arrow keys (↑ ↓ ← →) or WASD to change direction
  • Mobile: Swipe in the direction you want the snake to go
  • Goal: Eat the food to grow longer and score points
  • Don't crash: Avoid hitting walls or your own tail

The longer your snake gets, the harder it is to navigate without crashing. That's the core challenge that has kept players hooked since the Nokia era.

Snake Game Tips & High Score Strategies

1. Stay Near the Center

The edges are death traps. By staying toward the middle of the board, you give yourself more escape routes when your snake gets long.

2. Plan Your Path

Don't just chase food blindly. Look at where the food appears and plan a route that keeps you away from your own tail. Think 3-4 moves ahead.

3. Use the Zigzag Pattern

When your snake is long, move in a zigzag pattern across the board. This fills space efficiently and gives you room to maneuver.

4. Slow Down Early

In the beginning, take your time. There's no rush to grab food. Building good habits early pays off when the snake gets longer and faster.

5. Learn to Wrap

On boards where you can pass through walls, use wrapping strategically to escape tight situations.

The History of Snake

Snake traces its origins to the 1976 arcade game Blockade, but it became a global phenomenon when Nokia pre-loaded it on mobile phones in 1998. The Nokia 6110's Snake game became one of the most played video games in history, introducing billions to the simple joy of guiding a growing line around a grid. Today, Snake remains one of the most searched-for browser games worldwide.
