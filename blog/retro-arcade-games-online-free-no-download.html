Pac-Man is the world's most recognized video game. The original maze chase, perfected in 1980 and never meaningfully improved. Four ghosts, each with distinct AI behaviors — Blinky chases directly, Pinky flanks, Inky zigzags, and Clyde wanders. The result is emergent complexity from simple rules. **Pro tip:** The "米" pattern (drawn by the ghost house exit timing) lets you clear a section of the board while ghosts reset. ### Tetris — The Perfect Puzzle **How to play:** Rotate and drop falling blocks (tetrominoes) to complete horizontal lines. Complete lines disappear and earn points. Soviet game designer Alexey Pajitnov created Tetris in 1984. It has been called the most perfect puzzle game ever designed. The "足够" (enough) feeling when you chain multiple line clears is unmatched. **Pro tip:** Always look at the next piece preview. Plan placements two or three pieces ahead for the smoothest runs. ### Snake — The Phone Classic **How to play:** Eat dots to grow longer. Avoid hitting your own tail or the walls. Nokia pre-loaded Snake on mobile phones in 1998, making it the first video game billions of people ever played. Simple: eat, grow, don't die. Yet it remains endlessly compelling. **Pro tip:** Leave the corners open for escape routes. Snake deaths usually happen when you box yourself in. ### Space Invaders — The Original Shooter **How to play:** Move left-right and shoot descending alien invaders before they reach the bottom. Space Invaders defined the shooter genre in 1978. The descending alien formation, the descending bass pulse of its music, the terror of being overrun — Space Invaders created the template for every shooter that followed. ### Frogger — The Original Timing Game **How to play:** Navigate a frog across a busy road and a river to reach home slots. Frogger's one-screen, timing-based design is the ancestor of every mobile game that asks "how long can you survive?" One wrong jump and you're roadkill. Simple, brutal, perfect. --- ## The Arcade Cabinet Experience Online Modern browser technology has made these classics more faithful than ever: - **Pixel-perfect rendering** at original resolution - **Authentic sound effects** (the original chirps and bleeps) - **Leaderboards** — compete with players worldwide - **Mobile touch controls** — play anywhere - **No downloads** — click and play instantly --- ## Why Browser Arcade Games Are Better Than Emulators You might know emulators like RetroArch or MAME.
