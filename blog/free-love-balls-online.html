What Is Love Balls?

Love Balls is a charming brain-teaser puzzle where you help two love-struck balls reunite. The catch: they can't do it on their own. You draw lines, ramps, and bumpers on the playfield to guide one ball to the other across obstacles, gaps, spikes, and traps.

Each level gives you a starting ball, a target ball, and a fixed number of "ink" to draw with. Draw too much and you run out before solving it. Draw too little and the ball misses the goal. The sweet spot — the minimum line count that still works — is where the puzzle lives.

This draw-to-connect genre exploded on mobile in 2017 and remains hugely popular in 2026 because it's intuitive (anyone can draw a line) but the late-game puzzles demand real physics intuition.

How to Play Love Balls

The core loop is dead simple, but mastery takes practice:

  1. Look at the level — one ball is your starting point, the other is the goal.
  2. Plan a path — imagine a line from start to goal that avoids traps.
  3. Draw your line — press and hold (mouse or finger), drag to draw.
  4. Release to commit — the line becomes solid geometry.
  5. Tap to launch — the ball rolls and reacts to your lines with realistic physics.
  6. Iterate — if it fails, hit retry and draw a better path. Use fewer lines for a higher score.

Love Balls Strategy: 7 Tips to Earn 3 Stars

  1. Sketch the path in your head first. Don't draw until you see the route.
  2. Use ramps, not walls. Ramps give the ball momentum; walls waste ink.
  3. Bumpers beat barriers. A short angled line is often enough to redirect a ball.
  4. Steal momentum from gravity. Curves and slopes multiply a ball's speed for free.
  5. Test often. Don't draw 5 lines and hope; draw 1, test, then add more.
  6. Watch for hidden bounces. Sometimes a tiny line at the right angle does what a long ramp can't.
  7. Don't fear reset. Trying a fresh path is faster than tweaking a bad one.
