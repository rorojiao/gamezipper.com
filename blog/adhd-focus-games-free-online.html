Why These Games Help

Research in cognitive psychology suggests that games targeting working memory, response inhibition, and sustained attention can improve focus-related skills over time. A 2022 review in the Journal of Attention Disorders found that gamified cognitive training showed measurable improvements in attention span, especially when practiced in short daily sessions.

The games above aren't a substitute for professional treatment, but they're a free, accessible way to practice the same cognitive skills that ADHD affects. Think of them as 5-minute brain workouts you can do between tasks.

Tips for Maximum Benefit

  1. Short sessions — 5-10 minutes is ideal. Don't marathon; spread sessions throughout the day.
  2. Variety — rotate between puzzle, memory, and speed games to train different cognitive skills.
  3. Consistency — daily practice builds stronger neural pathways than occasional long sessions.
  4. No multitasking — give the game your full attention. That's the whole point of the exercise.
