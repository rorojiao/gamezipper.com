🎨 Color Sort Puzzle Tips & Strategy — Solve Every Level

Color Sort Puzzle looks simple — just sort colored liquids into tubes until each tube holds only one color. But once you hit level 10 and beyond, things get tricky fast. This guide covers proven strategies that work from beginner levels all the way to expert difficulty.

🧠 The Core Rules (Quick Refresher)

  • You can only pour the top color from one tube to another
  • You can pour onto a tube only if the top color matches or the tube is empty
  • A tube is "complete" when it holds only one color and is full
  • You have a limited number of empty tubes to use as temporary space

Strategy #1: The "One Color at a Time" Method

This is the most reliable strategy for beginners and intermediate players:

Step 1: Scan all tubes and pick the color that appears most on top of tubes. This is your "target color."

Step 2: Move all other colors OFF any tube that has your target color on top or near the top.

Step 3: Consolidate all of that target color into one tube until it's complete.

Step 4: Move to the next most common top color and repeat.

This method works because it gives you a clear focus. Instead of juggling 5 colors at once, you handle one and create more space for the rest.

Strategy #2: Keep Empty Tubes Sacred

⚠️ The #1 mistake: Filling up all your empty tubes early. Once you have zero empty tubes, every move is locked in and you're likely stuck.

Always keep at least one empty tube as your "overflow" space. Think of it like a spare hand in a card game — you need it to shuffle things around. Here's how:

  • Never pour a mixed tube into your last empty tube unless it completes a color
  • Use empty tubes to temporarily hold colors while you rearrange
  • If you're down to 0 empty tubes and stuck, undo — don't restart yet

Strategy #3: Think 3 Moves Ahead

Advanced players don't just look at the current move. Before pouring, ask yourself:

  • What will I do with the color I'm covering up?
  • Will this move create a new complete tube, or just shift the problem?
  • Is there a sequence of 2-3 moves that completes a tube?

If a move doesn't either (a) complete a tube or (b) set up a completion in the next move, it's probably wasting your empty tube space.

Strategy #4: The "Bottom-Up" Scan

Instead of just looking at top colors, scan from the bottom of each tube:

  1. Find a tube where the bottom color is nearly complete (3 out of 4 slots are the same color)
  2. Prioritize getting that tube finished — it frees up a whole tube
  3. This is especially powerful in later levels with 10+ tubes

Strategy #5: When to Use Undo vs Restart

Every Color Sort game lets you undo moves. Use it wisely:

  • Undo (1-5 moves): When you realize a recent move created a deadlock
  • Undo (10+ moves): When you can trace back to where things went wrong
  • Restart: When you've been undoing for a while and still can't find a path forward. Fresh eyes help!

📊 Difficulty Progression Tips

Levels 1-5 (Easy)

Just practice the basic mechanics. Focus on consolidating one color at a time. You have plenty of empty tubes, so don't overthink it.

Levels 6-15 (Medium)

Colors increase and empty tubes decrease. Start using the "One Color at a Time" method strictly. Keep an empty tube free at all times.

Levels 16+ (Hard)

Use the "Bottom-Up Scan" and think 3+ moves ahead. Every move matters. Don't be afraid to undo frequently — even top players undo 10+ times per level.
