How to Play Snake

The History of Snake — From Nokia to Browser

The Snake game has been around since the 1970s, but it became a global phenomenon when Nokia pre-loaded it on mobile phones in 1998. Over 400 million people played Snake on their Nokia phones, making it one of the most-played games in history. Google paid tribute to this legacy with their interactive Snake doodle, which introduced a new generation to the addictive gameplay.

Today, the Snake game lives on in browsers everywhere. Modern HTML5 versions like ours run smoothly on any device without downloads or plugins. Whether you remember the pixelated Nokia version or discovered Snake through Google's doodle, the core appeal remains the same: simple rules, deep strategy, and that "one more try" feeling.

Snake Game Strategies — Beginner to Advanced

Beginner Strategy: The Wall Hug

Start by keeping your snake close to the edges of the board. This leaves the center open for maneuvering and reduces the chance of trapping yourself. As a beginner, focus on surviving rather than scoring — the points will come naturally as you play more.

Intermediate Strategy: The Spiral

Once you're comfortable, try the spiral approach: move in increasingly tight circles from the outside in. This keeps your path predictable and leaves maximum room for the snake to grow. The spiral method is especially effective on larger grids.

Advanced Strategy: The Safe Zone

Expert players maintain a "safe zone" — an area of the board they never enter unless necessary. By keeping a portion of the board completely clear, you always have an escape route when the snake gets long. This is how top players achieve scores of 300+.
