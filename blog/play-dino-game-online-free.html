How to Play the Dino Game Online

Playing the Dino Game is simple — but mastering it takes practice:

  • SPACE or UP ARROW — Jump (also TAP on mobile)
  • DOWN ARROW — Duck under pterodactyls (when they appear at higher speeds)
  • Avoid cacti of all sizes — they end your run instantly
  • Watch out for pterodactyls — they fly at two heights, jump or duck accordingly
  • The game speeds up the longer you survive
  • Your high score is saved locally on your device

Dino Game Tips & Tricks to Beat Your High Score

🏃 Start slow: Many players rush at the beginning and hit a cactus within seconds. Take the first few seconds to get into a rhythm before picking up speed.

1. Jump Timing is Everything

The key to a high score in the Dino Game is predicting obstacles early. Cacti appear in patterns — learn to recognize the spacing. A good rule: jump when a cactus is about 1.5 dinosaur lengths away.

2. Use the Sound Cues

When a pterodactyl approaches, you'll hear a distinctive sound. This gives you time to decide: jump or duck? At lower speeds, pterodactyls fly high — just keep running. At higher speeds, they fly low — you need to duck (DOWN ARROW).

3. The 700-Point Milestone

Around 700 points, the game hits a noticeable speed increase. Be prepared! This is where most runs end. Practice surviving past 700 to dramatically improve your scores.

4. Night Mode Trick

The Dino Game automatically toggles between day and night. In night mode, the cacti and pterodactyls are harder to see against the dark background. Some players find day mode slightly easier for this reason.

5. Practice Makes Perfect

Like any skill-based game, the Dino Game rewards muscle memory. Play regularly, focus on consistency over risky moves, and you'll naturally improve. Many players reach 500+ points within a week of regular play.
