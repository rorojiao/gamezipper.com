How to Play Brick Breaker

  1. Move the paddle — Use your mouse, finger (mobile), or arrow keys to move the paddle left and right
  2. Bounce the ball — Keep the ball in play by bouncing it off your paddle
  3. Break the bricks — The ball destroys bricks on contact. Clear all bricks to advance
  4. Catch power-ups — Some bricks drop power-ups: wider paddle, multi-ball, fireball, and more
  5. Don't miss the ball! — If the ball falls below your paddle, you lose a life

Brick Breaker Tips and Strategy

  • Aim for the top — Clearing top bricks early creates cascading breaks
  • Catch every power-up — Especially the wide paddle and multi-ball
  • Control the angle — Hit the ball with different parts of the paddle to change its angle
  • Watch for falling power-ups — Don't lose the ball while chasing a power-up
  • Stay calm at high speed — As bricks break, the ball speeds up. Keep steady

History of Brick Breaker / Breakout

Breakout was designed by Nolan Bushnell and Steve Wozniak at Atari in 1976. The concept was simple but addictive: a single player uses a paddle to bounce a ball into rows of bricks. The game was directly inspired by Pong — essentially a single-player version of tennis.

Over the decades, Brick Breaker has appeared on nearly every gaming platform: arcade cabinets, Game Boy, mobile phones (BlackBerry Brick Breaker was legendary), and now web browsers. The core gameplay remains as addictive today as it was 50 years ago.
