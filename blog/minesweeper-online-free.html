How to Play Minesweeper

The rules of Minesweeper are simple but the strategy runs deep:

  • Click a cell to reveal what's underneath. Numbers show how many mines are adjacent to that cell.
  • Right-click (or long-press on mobile) to flag a cell you think contains a mine.
  • Use logic — if a "1" only has one unrevealed neighbor, that neighbor is a mine!
  • Clear all safe cells to win the game.

Minesweeper Tips for Beginners

  1. Start in corners — clicking a corner gives you more information with fewer adjacent cells.
  2. Look for 1-1 patterns — when two "1"s share a neighbor, the mine must be in one specific spot.
  3. Flag first, then deduce — mark obvious mines before trying to figure out tricky spots.
  4. Don't guess — in a well-designed game, you can always deduce the answer. If you must guess, pick a cell far from the action.

The History of Minesweeper — From Windows to Browser

Minesweeper has been one of the most recognized computer games since Microsoft included it with Windows 3.1 in 1992. For over a decade, it was the go-to office time killer, pre-installed on every Windows PC. The game teaches logical deduction — each number tells you how many mines touch that cell, and with careful reasoning you can clear the entire board without guessing.

The original Microsoft Minesweeper had two difficulty levels: Beginner (9×9, 10 mines) and Expert (30×16, 99 mines). Our browser version lets you enjoy the same classic gameplay on any device — no Windows required. Whether you're on a Chromebook, tablet, or phone, the puzzles are just as satisfying.

Minesweeper Tips for Beginners

  1. Start with corners and edges — these cells have fewer neighbors, making them easier to deduce.
  2. Look for 1-2-1 patterns — when you see this sequence along an edge, the mines are always next to the 1s.
  3. Flag before clicking — marking suspected mines helps you visualize safe cells.
  4. Don't guess — in most cases, logic alone can solve the puzzle. If you must guess, pick a cell with the lowest probability of being a mine.
