How to Play

Click or tap to make your bird flap upward. Release and gravity pulls you down.

Navigate through the gaps between pipes. Each successful pass scores +1. Hit a pipe or the ground and it's game over.

The twist: The bird falls fast, and the pipes are always slightly different heights. No two rounds are the same.

Why Flappy Bird Became a Sensation

When Flappy Bird launched in 2013, it was downloaded over 50 million times before being pulled from app stores by its creator (who said it was too addictive). The combination of:

  • Simple one-button controls
  • High difficulty curve
  • "Just one more try" compulsion
  • Easy sharing of high scores

...created the perfect viral game. It spawned thousands of clones, memes, and even a slot machine adaptation.

Tips to Beat Your High Score

1. Don't Hold — Tap Rhythmically

Think of tapping like breathing. Consistent, rhythmic taps keep you in control. One hard hold will send you skyward into a pipe.

2. Aim for the Center of Gaps

Don't aim for the bottom of the gap or the top — aim for the middle. Pipes are tall, and you need buffer room.

3. Watch the First Pipe

The difficulty ramps up quickly. Most players die on the first few pipes. Master the opening rhythm before worrying about later pipes.

4. Use the Pipes' Positioning

Pipes tend to alternate between high-left/low-right and low-left/high-right. Use this pattern to anticipate.

5. Flap Earlier Than You Think

Novices always flap too late. Your reaction time has a delay — flap slightly before you think you need to.

The Psychology of Flappy Bird

The game exploits a specific cognitive loop:

  1. Near-miss feedback — you almost made it past that pipe
  2. Variable reward — pipes are always slightly different
  3. Loss aversion — you've come so close, you can't quit now
  4. Social comparison — everyone is sharing their embarrassing low scores

This is why Flappy Bird clones still get millions of plays today. The formula works.
