Games Like NYT Connections — Best Free Connections Alternatives 2026

Updated April 6, 2026 · 6 min read

If you love the daily challenge of NYT Connections — that satisfying moment when you spot the hidden word between 16 seemingly random words — you'll love these free alternatives. All work instantly in your browser, no download needed.

🎯 What Is NYT Connections?

Connections is The New York Times' popular daily word puzzle where you group 16 words into 4 categories. Each puzzle has one easy group, one medium, one hard, and one "spicy" (tricky) group. It's deceptively simple but genuinely addictive — the daily ritual millions look forward to.

🔝 Top Games Like Connections

1. Connections Unlimited

Play unlimited NYT Connections-style puzzles with past archives. No daily limit — great for practice and building pattern recognition skills. Works on any device.

2. Connections Game

A clean recreation of the Connections format with a community archive of past puzzles. Lets you see mistakes and track your solve stats over time.

3. Spelling Bee (NYT)

If you haven't tried Spelling Bee, it's the NYT's other daily word puzzle. You get 7 letters and must form as many words as possible using only those letters. Different skill, same addiction.

4. Wordle Unlimited

Not quite Connections, but Wordle is the game that started the daily word puzzle craze. Play unlimited rounds free on GameZipper with no download needed.

5. Mini Crossword (NYT)

A smaller 5x5 daily crossword puzzle from NYT. Quick to solve but still satisfying. Free in your browser if you're willing to see ads, or through NYT Cooking app.

🧩 More Connections-Style Games

6. Connections Junior

A kid-friendly version of Connections with simpler words and topics. Great for younger word puzzle fans learning pattern recognition.

7. Primel

For math lovers — guess a 5-digit prime number in 6 tries. Uses Wordle-style feedback with green, yellow, and gray indicators. A clever numerical twist on the daily puzzle format.

8. Anti-Pattern

A pattern-recognition game where you figure out what connects a set of images. More visual than Connections but shares that same "aha!" moment when you spot the link.

9. Squirdle

For Pokemon fans — guess a Pokemon using clues about type, generation, and height/weight. Another Wordle-style clone with a creative twist that word puzzle lovers enjoy.

10. Nerdle

Math Wordle — guess the hidden calculation instead of a word. Uses number tiles instead of letters. Perfect for people who love the number-crunching side of puzzles.

💡 How to Get Better at Connections-Style Puzzles

  • Look for odd words first — words that don't seem to fit anywhere are often in the hard or spicy category
  • Think categories, not just words — try to imagine the four category names before solving
  • Use the shuffle — rearranging the letters can help you see patterns you missed
  • Practice with past puzzles — sites like ConnectionsUnlimited have hundreds of archived puzzles
  • Learn common trap categories — "Things that are X" and "X and Y" are frequent tricky categories

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Are these Connections alternatives free?

Yes, all the games and sites listed here are completely free to play in your browser. No subscription, no download, no registration required for most.

Can I play Connections if I miss a day?

NYT Connections only lets you play the current day's puzzle. But sites like ConnectionsUnlimited let you play past archived puzzles so you're never stuck waiting for tomorrow.

What's harder than Connections?

The "spicy" or purple category in Connections is the hardest level. Once you master daily Connections, try ConnectionsUnlimited's harder archive puzzles for a real challenge.

Is there an app for Connections?

The NYT Connections game is part of the New York Times Games app (iOS/Android). The app is free to download but requires a NYT Games subscription to play all games after a trial period.
