What Is Screw Jam?

Screw Jam (also known as Nuts & Bolts Puzzle or Screw Pin Jam) is a satisfying 3D physics puzzle where colored bolts are screwed into wooden boards and your job is to unscrew them in the right order so all the wooden plates fall to the ground. If you unscrew a bolt that's holding up a plate, the plate falls. If you unscrew a bolt that's not holding up a plate, the plate stays stuck — and now you're stuck too.

The genre exploded in 2024–2026 thanks to mobile hits like Screw Master and Unscrew Jam, and it stays popular because the tactile satisfaction of twisting a bolt out and watching a plate clatter down is genuinely addictive. It belongs to the same "calm logic" family as sliding puzzles, tangled yarn, and color sort.

How to Play Screw Jam

The rules are simple to learn but the levels can get deviously tricky. Here's the full breakdown:

  1. Look at the stack. Each level shows wooden plates with colored bolts sticking out the side.
  2. Tap a bolt to unscrew it — it falls away from the board.
  3. Watch what happens. If the bolt was the only thing holding a plate in place, the plate falls to the ground.
  4. Goal: unscrew every bolt so that every plate falls. The level is complete when no plate is left floating.
  5. There's no time limit and no score. Just figure out the right order, then move to the next level.

Tips for Solving Screw Jam Faster

  1. Always start from the bottom. The bottom plate of the stack usually has the fewest constraints — find the bolts holding it up and unscrew them first.
  2. Look for "free" bolts. A bolt with no plate above it is always safe to unscrew. Get all the free ones out of the way first.
  3. Same-color bolts are usually independent. In most levels, bolts of the same color don't pin each other down. Unscrew them in any order.
  4. Don't unscrew the "keystone" bolt. Some levels have one bolt that's the only thing holding up an entire stack. Don't touch it until you've cleared the surrounding plates.
  5. Use undo aggressively. If a plate falls in the wrong order and traps another plate, undo and try a different sequence.
  6. Think visually, not logically. Screw Jam is more "spatial reasoning" than "deduction" — just look at the stack and trace which bolt holds which plate.
  7. Zoom out on hard levels. Some levels have 6+ plates — pull back to see the full stack before committing to a move.
