What Is Jewel Coloring?

Jewel coloring (also called jewel art or diamond painting game) is a relaxing color-by-number pixel-art puzzle. Each canvas is a grid of cells, and every cell has a tiny number. Tap a color in the palette, then tap any cell with that number — a sparkling jewel drops into it. As you fill more cells, a hidden picture gradually emerges from the blank grid. The result looks like a glittering mosaic.

The genre exploded in 2020–2022 with apps like Happy Color and Pixel Art, and it stays popular because it's the most meditative puzzle genre on mobile: no timers, no scores, no wrong moves, just a satisfying tap-tap-reveal rhythm. It belongs to the same "calm creation" family as sliding puzzles and tangled yarn.

How to Play Jewel Coloring

If you've ever done a paint-by-numbers kit, you already know how to play. Here's the browser version:

  1. Pick a picture from the 30+ available artworks across 6 categories.
  2. Choose a color from the palette at the bottom of the screen.
  3. Tap any cell with a matching number — a jewel appears in that cell.
  4. Switch colors as needed — most pictures use 8–15 colors.
  5. Use the hint button if you lose track of where you are on the canvas.
  6. Reveal the picture — once you fill all the cells, the finished artwork glows.

Tips for Jewel Coloring Faster (and More Enjoyably)

  1. Start with the most common color. The palette always has one or two colors that fill 30%+ of the canvas. Do those first — you'll see the picture emerge quickly.
  2. Group by region. Instead of switching colors constantly, finish all the cells of one color before moving to the next.
  3. Use the zoom on dense sections. A 30×30 cell canvas is hard to tap accurately on a phone — zoom in for tight clusters.
  4. Don't worry about the order. Unlike sudoku, jewel coloring has no constraint on which cell you fill first. Just tap and enjoy.
  5. Use hints sparingly. Most players only need the hint button on huge 50×50 pictures.
  6. Take breaks between pictures. A finished jewel picture is genuinely satisfying — give yourself a moment to look at it before starting the next.
  7. Pick categories that match your mood. Animals for cozy days, abstract for focus sessions, fantasy for creative bursts.
