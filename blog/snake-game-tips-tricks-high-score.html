Snake Game Tips & Tricks: How to Beat Your High Score

April 8, 2026 · 8 min read

The snake game has been around since the Nokia 6110 in 1997, and yet it remains one of the most surprisingly deep arcade games ever created. It looks effortless — move a growing line, eat food, avoid walls and yourself. But anyone who has played seriously knows that the game becomes exponentially harder as your snake fills more of the screen. One wrong turn and it's over.

That's exactly why we've put together this guide. Whether you're chasing your first 50 points or your personal best of 500, these snake game tips and tricks will help you play smarter, survive longer, and score higher.

1. Master the Wall-Hugging Technique

The Single Most Effective Snake Game Strategy

When your snake is short, the board feels wide open. But as it grows, navigation becomes a real challenge. The wall-hugging technique solves this by keeping your snake along the edges of the board, leaving the open center free for movement.

Instead of cutting across the middle and potentially trapping yourself, follow the perimeter. This approach is particularly powerful when your snake gets long — it dramatically reduces the chance of boxing yourself into a corner with no escape route.

When to use it: Start wall-hugging early, before the board gets crowded. It sets up a navigation habit that pays off huge once your snake is 10+ segments long.

2. Always Leave Yourself an Escape Route

The #1 Reason Players Die

The most common mistake in the snake game is rushing to grab food and accidentally closing off your own escape path. Before you make any move, ask yourself: "Where can I go after this?" If the answer is "only one place," you're setting yourself up for a collision.

A good habit is to visualize your snake's body as a series of future obstacles. Each segment will be in a different position in a few moves — plan accordingly. This is essentially pathfinding, and even basic pathfinding dramatically improves survival rates.

3. Use Food Trapping to Your Advantage

Make the Food Come to You

Sometimes food spawns in an awkward spot — wedged between your body and the wall, or in a tight corner. Instead of forcing a risky grab, try to "trap" the food in a safe position first. Move your snake so that the only approach to the food is a clean, controlled path.

This technique is especially useful on larger boards where food can end up far from your current position. Instead of racing across the board and potentially crossing your own tail, maneuver your snake to intercept the food from a safe direction. Patience is the key here.

4. Control Your Speed (When You Can)

Slow Down When It Matters Most

Many snake game versions allow you to control the snake's speed, either directly or indirectly. In games where the snake speeds up with each food item eaten, the moments right after eating are the most dangerous — the snake has just grown, the board is tighter, and the pace is faster.

If your game allows any speed control, use it strategically. Slow down during tight navigation sections. Speed up during open stretches where there's plenty of room. This simple awareness separates good players from great ones.

5. Plan for Your Tail, Not Just Your Head

Think in Sequences, Not Single Moves

Novice players react to the present — they move away from immediate threats. Experienced players think in sequences. They know that the space their head currently occupies will be empty in a few moves, but the space near their tail will be occupied for longer.

A useful mental model: imagine your snake as a moving wall. As you move forward, the tail "frees up" space behind it. Track where your tail segments will be in 5-10 moves, and use that to plan your current route.

6. The Corner Control Method

Use Corners as Navigation Anchors

When the board gets very full, experienced players use corners as anchor points. Instead of trying to navigate open space (which can become unpredictable), guide your snake into a corner and use the two walls to control your movement direction.

This method works because corners limit your available directions to just two — up/down or left/right. With fewer choices, it's harder to accidentally make a fatal wrong turn. Once you've settled into a corner rhythm, you can carefully extend back into the open board without risking a head-on collision with your own body.

7. Practice Makes Permanent, Not Just Perfect

The Long Game Approach

One of the most overlooked snake game tips is simply this: play regularly. The game has a surprisingly high skill ceiling, and the players with the highest scores have usually logged hundreds of games. The spatial awareness and pathfinding instincts that make a great snake player develop over time.

Start a session with the goal of beating your previous high score. Even small improvements — surviving 30 seconds longer, reaching a score 5 points higher — compound over time. Before you know it, your new "normal" will be what used to be your personal best.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Chasing food blindly. It can be tempting to make a beeline for food the moment you see it. Resist this impulse. Food isn't going anywhere, and a risky grab that ends your run isn't worth it.

Moving too fast in tight spaces. When your snake fills half the board, every move matters. Slow down and think through each direction change.

Forgetting your own tail. As your snake grows, its body forms increasingly complex shapes. Always know where every segment is before making a move.

Getting cocky after a big score. The moment you feel confident is often the moment you make a careless mistake. Stay focused even when you're dominating the board.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best snake game tips for beginners?

The most important snake game tips for beginners are: control your speed, never rush into open space, and start using the wall-hugging technique early. Focus on survival first, score second — the longer you stay alive, the higher your score naturally climbs.

What is the wall-hugging technique in snake game?

Wall-hugging means keeping your snake along the edges of the board as you grow. This leaves the open center space free for navigation, which reduces the chance of accidentally boxing yourself in. It's one of the most effective survival strategies in classic snake gameplay.

How do I get a high score on the snake game?

To get a high score, master these three things: (1) efficient pathfinding so you don't waste movement on dead-end detours, (2) food trapping — cornering food in a spot where you can grab it safely, and (3) maintaining a long snake without dying, which requires leaving escape routes open at all times.

Is there a trick to not dying in the snake game?

The core trick is never to move into a space your body will need in the next few moves. Always maintain at least one escape route. When the board gets crowded, slow down if the game allows speed control, and use the walls and your own body as navigation guides rather than obstacles.
