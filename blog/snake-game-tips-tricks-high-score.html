Snake Game Tips & Tricks: How to Get a High Score Every Time

Published April 2, 2026 · 5 min read

The classic Snake game is one of the most addictive browser games ever made. Simple to learn, hard to master. Whether you're playing on your phone during a break or killing time on your laptop, these tips will help you dramatically improve your score.

🎯 1. The Edge-Hugging Strategy

The single most effective Snake strategy is to move along the edges of the board in a consistent clockwise or counter-clockwise pattern. This ensures your tail stays on the perimeter and the center remains open for maneuvering when the snake gets long.

🐍 2. Never Chase Your Tail

When food spawns near your tail, don't rush straight for it. Approach from the outside so you don't create a coil that traps you. Patience beats greed in Snake.

⌨️ 3. Use Keyboard Shortcuts

On desktop, use arrow keys for precise control. On mobile, use swipe gestures with deliberate, slow movements — fast swipes cause accidental direction changes. Play Snake free in your browser here.

🧠 4. Think Two Moves Ahead

Don't just react to where the food is. Before making your move, ask: "After I eat this, where will my tail be?" This single habit prevents 80% of self-collision deaths.

⚡ 5. Speed Control

In most Snake versions, the snake speeds up as it grows. Early game, play fast to rack up points. Once you're past 50 points, slow down and prioritize survival over speed.

🔲 6. The Zigzag Pattern

For advanced players, the zigzag pattern involves moving left-right across the entire width of the board, dropping down one row each time you reach an edge. This creates a predictable path that's almost impossible to self-trap with.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the highest possible score in Snake?

In theory, the highest score fills the entire grid. On a standard 20×20 grid that's about 399 points. Most players consider 100+ an excellent score.

Is there a trick to the Snake game?

The best trick is the "edge hugging" strategy: always move along the borders in a consistent pattern, leaving the center open so you never trap yourself.

Why do I keep dying in Snake?

Most deaths happen from turning into your own body. Avoid sudden direction changes in tight spaces and always plan 2-3 moves ahead.
