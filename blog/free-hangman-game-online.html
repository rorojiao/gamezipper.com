What Is Hangman?

Hangman is the classic word-guessing game that's been played on chalkboards and notebooks for over a century. One player thinks of a secret word and reveals how many letters it has (e.g. _ _ _ _ _). The other player guesses one letter at a time. Correct guesses fill in the blanks. Wrong guesses draw another body part on the gallows. You have 6 wrong guesses — solve the word before the figure is complete or you lose the round.

Hangman is one of the few games that's been played continuously since the 1890s — by grandparents in pencil-and-paper form, by kids in classrooms, and now by anyone with a browser. It belongs to the same "calm vocabulary" family as Wordscapes, Sudoku, and Wordle.

Tips for Winning More Hangman Rounds

  1. Start with common vowels. A, E, I, O, U. This is Hangman 101 — vowels appear in almost every English word, and they reveal the word's structure fastest.
  2. Then common consonants. After vowels, guess T, N, R, S, L — these are the most common consonants in English.
  3. Look at the letter pattern. Once you've guessed a few vowels, the word's pattern is often obvious: _ A _ E _ might be PAPER, LATER, EATER, etc.
  4. Use the category hint. If the category is "Animals" and you see _ I _ _ _, you're looking at TIGER, RIVER (no, that's not an animal...), HIPPO, etc.
  5. Don't waste guesses on rare letters. Q, X, Z, J are usually last priorities — they're expensive to guess wrong.
  6. Watch the body count. After 4 wrong guesses you only have 2 left — switch to high-probability letters.
  7. Think about bigrams. TH, HE, IN, ER, AN — common letter pairs. If you've guessed T and see it could fit, E or H is a smart next guess.
  8. Don't be afraid to skip. If you're truly stuck after 4 wrong guesses, the "next" button loses you one round but saves your streak.
