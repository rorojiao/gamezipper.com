What Is Tidy Up 3D?

Tidy Up 3D is a satisfying 3D sorting puzzle where you take a chaotic pile of objects — books, soda cans, shirts, dishes, toys — and tidy them into perfect stacks. Every level has a goal: match objects by type, color, or category, and fit them all into the target containers before space runs out.

What sets Tidy Up 3D apart from older 2D sort games is the physical depth. Objects sit in real 3D space. They have weight, shadows, and satisfying snap-into-place animations. The audio is tuned for ASMR — soft clicks, gentle thuds, and clean chord feedback as you finish a stack.

This genre — sometimes called "satisfying sort" or "ASMR tidy" — has surged in mobile gaming since 2022. Players love the way it mimics the calm of organizing a real shelf or drawer, but with the instant gratification of a perfectly solved puzzle.

How to Play Tidy Up 3D

The basics are simple, the late levels require planning:

  1. Read the goal — each level tells you what to organize (e.g. "Sort the laundry by color").
  2. Pick an object — tap or click any item in the messy pile.
  3. Place it in the right container — drag or tap the matching stack.
  4. Match the rules — by type, color, size, or category, depending on the level.
  5. Don't run out of space — each container has a cap; mis-sorts waste slots.
  6. Beat the level — clear all objects within the limit to unlock the next.

Tidy Up 3D Strategy: 7 Tips to Beat Tricky Levels

  1. Read the goal three times. Some levels sort by color, others by category — don't mix them up.
  2. Start with the rarest items. If there are only 2 red shirts, place them first; they'll never clutter a wrong stack.
  3. Don't fill one stack early. Empty slots mid-game give you flexibility to fix mistakes.
  4. Use the camera angle. Rotate the view to see buried items — they're often the key to finishing.
  5. Plan two moves ahead. Late levels punish impulsive picks; think before you tap.
  6. Watch the counter. Most levels show remaining slots per stack — use it as a mental constraint.
  7. Don't fear a restart. Re-doing a level with a better plan is faster than struggling.
