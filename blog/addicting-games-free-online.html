🧠 Why Are These Games So Addictive?

The science behind addictive games comes down to a few key psychological principles:

The "Just One More" Loop

Each round is short (30 seconds to 2 minutes), which tricks your brain into thinking "I have time for one more." Before you know it, an hour has passed.

Variable Rewards

Games like 2048 and Color Sort use variable difficulty — sometimes you succeed easily, sometimes you struggle. This unpredictability triggers dopamine release, the same mechanism behind slot machine addiction.

Clear Progression

Numbers going up (score, level, upgrades) provides constant positive feedback. Your brain loves watching metrics improve, even in simple games.

Instant Restart

No loading screens, no wait times. The moment you fail, you can try again. This eliminates the "cool down" period where you might otherwise decide to stop playing.

Low Barrier, High Ceiling

All these games take 5 seconds to learn but hours to master. This combination keeps both beginners and experts engaged.
