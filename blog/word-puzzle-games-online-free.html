Why Word Puzzle Games Are Great for Your Brain

Research published in the journal NEJM Evidence showed that people who regularly do word puzzles perform better on cognitive tests. Here's what word games help with:

  • Vocabulary expansion — Encounter new words and reinforce spelling
  • Pattern recognition — Train your brain to spot letter combinations quickly
  • Memory improvement — Recall words from your mental dictionary faster
  • Stress relief — Focused puzzle-solving is a form of mindfulness
  • Cognitive flexibility — Switch between different word strategies on the fly

Tips to Improve at Word Puzzles

  • Start with short words — 2-3 letter words are easier to spot and help you see patterns
  • Look for common prefixes — RE-, UN-, IN-, and suffixes like -ING, -ED, -LY
  • Try vowel combinations — Look for EA, OU, IE, AI patterns
  • Shuffle the letters — A fresh arrangement often reveals hidden words
  • Play daily — Even 10 minutes a day sharpens your word-finding skills
