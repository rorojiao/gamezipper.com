What Makes a Game a "Sticky Note" Game?

Sticky note games share a few key traits:

  • Instant start — No loading screens, no tutorials, no sign-up. You play immediately.
  • Short sessions — Designed for 3-5 minute play windows. Perfect for coffee breaks.
  • Easy to pause — Each game fits into a natural stopping point. No penalty for closing mid-game.
  • No commitment — No save files, no progression walls, no "you'll lose everything if you quit."
  • Works anywhere — Runs in any modern browser, including on work or school computers.

🐍 Snake — The Ultimate Sticky Note Game

Snake is the original sticky note game. One rule: eat food, grow longer, don't crash. You can play one round in 90 seconds or spend an hour chasing high scores. It's on every computer that ever existed and has been keeping people entertained since 1976.

  • Time per round: 1-3 minutes
  • Skill ceiling: Surprisingly deep — speed increases as you grow
  • Works on: Desktop, mobile, tablet

🧱 Brick Breaker — Satisfying Destruction in Minutes

Brick Breaker is perfect when you need something rhythmic and satisfying. Bounce a ball off a paddle to smash rows of bricks. One session can clear 10 waves or just 2 — there's no wrong amount of time to spend breaking bricks.

  • Time per round: 2-5 minutes
  • Skill ceiling: Medium — angle control takes practice
  • Works on: Desktop, mobile, tablet

🧩 2048 — The Number Puzzle Sticky Note

2048 is mathematically elegant — slide numbered tiles, combine matching numbers to reach 2048. Each game lasts 2-10 minutes depending on your skill level. It's addictive enough to play for hours but forgiving enough for a single coffee break.

  • Time per round: 2-10 minutes
  • Skill ceiling: High — optimal tile placement strategies exist
  • Works on: Desktop, mobile, tablet

📦 Stacker — Timing Precision in 60 Seconds

Stacker is the definition of a sticky note game: one screen, one mechanic, 60 seconds. Drop blocks to build a tower as it oscillates back and forth. The higher you stack, the harder it gets. One round is exactly one coffee break.

  • Time per round: Under 1 minute
  • Skill ceiling: High — requires precise timing
  • Works on: Desktop, mobile, tablet
