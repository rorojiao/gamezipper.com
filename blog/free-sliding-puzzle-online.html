What Is a Sliding Puzzle?

A sliding puzzle is one of the oldest and most satisfying brain games ever invented. The classic version — sometimes called the 15-puzzle or n-puzzle — is a 4×4 grid of numbered tiles (1 through 15) with one empty slot. You slide tiles into the empty space, one at a time, until the numbers are arranged in order. Sounds simple. Trying to solve one is a humbling lesson in planning and patience.

The sliding puzzle has been around since the 1870s and was one of the most popular puzzle toys of the 20th century. It went viral again in the smartphone era — partly because the touch screen is the perfect input device, and partly because everyone secretly wants to prove they can solve one.

How to Play Sliding Puzzle

Sliding puzzle is one of those games you can learn in 10 seconds and spend a lifetime trying to master. Here's how it works:

  1. Tap or click any tile adjacent to the empty space — it slides into the empty slot.
  2. Use arrow keys on desktop to slide tiles in the matching direction.
  3. Plan ahead — every move changes the position of multiple tiles.
  4. Don't trap the empty slot — if you can't move the tile you want, the empty space is in the wrong corner.
  5. Solve row by row — most players finish the top row first, then the second row, then the bottom two together.
  6. Track your moves — fewer moves = higher score.

Tips for Solving a Sliding Puzzle Faster

  1. Solve the top row first. Get 1, 2, 3, 4 into place. Once locked, don't move them.
  2. Then the second row. Same approach — get 5, 6, 7, 8 into place and leave them alone.
  3. The bottom two rows go together. Most of your time will be spent here.
  4. Keep the empty slot in the top-left of the working area. This is the most efficient position.
  5. Count your moves. A perfect 15-puzzle solve takes 80 moves or less. Average players use 100-200.
  6. Don't random-click. Every move should be part of a plan.
