12 Hidden Google Games You Can Play Free Right Now (2026)

Google has hidden dozens of free games inside Search, Chrome, Android, and other products. Most people never find them. Here are the best ones — and how to unlock each one in seconds. No downloads, no sign-ups, completely free.

1. T-Rex Dino Runner (Chrome Offline Game)

The most famous hidden Google game. When your internet goes down in Chrome, a pixelated T-Rex appears. Press spacebar (or tap on mobile) to start running and jumping over cacti and pterodactyls. The game speeds up the longer you survive. To play it anytime — even with internet — type chrome://dino in your Chrome address bar.

2. Pac-Man (Google Search)

Search "Pac-Man" on Google and a playable Pac-Man game appears at the top of results. Use arrow keys to navigate the maze, eat dots, and avoid ghosts — just like the classic arcade. It's a faithful recreation of the original.

3. Snake Game (Google Search)

Search "snake game" and Google shows a built-in snake game. Use arrow keys to eat apples and grow your snake without hitting walls or your own tail. Want a better snake experience? Try our Snake game on GameZipper — it has mobile swipe controls, high score tracking, and a cleaner interface.

4. Solitaire (Google Search)

Search "solitaire" and Google's built-in version appears. Choose easy or hard difficulty. Drag cards to build foundations by suit from Ace to King. It tracks your time and moves.

5. Minesweeper (Google Search)

Search "minesweeper" to play Google's version. Click squares to reveal numbers, use logic to flag mines. Choose beginner, intermediate, or expert difficulty.

6. Tic-Tac-Toe (Google Search)

Search "tic tac toe" and play against Google's AI. Choose easy, medium, or impossible difficulty. Great for a quick 30-second brain break.

7. Flight Simulator (Google Earth)

Open Google Earth Pro on desktop, press Ctrl+Alt+A (Windows) or Cmd+Option+A (Mac) to launch a hidden flight simulator. Fly over real satellite imagery with an F-16 or SR22 aircraft. It supports joystick controls too.

8. Atari Breakout (Google Image Search)

Go to Google Images and search "Atari Breakout." The image results transform into a playable Breakout game. Move your paddle to bounce the ball and destroy bricks. Classic arcade fun.

9. Zerg Rush (Google Search)

Search "zerg rush" on Google and red and yellow Os start falling from the top of the screen, eating your search results. Click them rapidly to destroy them before they consume everything. A fun StarCraft easter egg.

10. Android Easter Egg (Settings)

On your Android phone, go to Settings → About Phone → tap "Android version" repeatedly. Each Android version has a different hidden mini-game. Android 14 features a space-themed game. Previous versions had Neko (cat collector), bubble paint, and more.

11. Google Assistant Games

Say "Hey Google, play a game" to your Google Assistant. Choose from trivia, word games, crystal ball, and more. Works on phones and smart speakers. Great for families and kids.

12. Google Doodle Games Archive

Google has created hundreds of interactive Doodle games for special occasions. Visit google.com/doodles to browse the archive. Highlights include the 2017 cricket game, 2020 baseball, and the coding rabbit game.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Google have hidden games?

Yes! Google has hidden several games inside its products, including the T-Rex Dino Runner in Chrome, Pac-Man and Snake in Google Search, and Solitaire and Minesweeper in Google Search results. This guide covers 12 of the best ones.

How do I play the Google T-Rex game?

Open Chrome, disconnect from the internet, and you'll see the T-Rex Dino Runner. Press the spacebar to start jumping over cacti. You can also visit chrome://dino anytime — even with internet connected.

Are Google hidden games free?

Yes, all hidden Google games are completely free to play. No downloads, no sign-ups, and no ads. Just open your browser and start playing.

Can I play Google games on my phone?

Most Google hidden games work on mobile browsers. The T-Rex Dino game, Snake, and Solitaire all support touch controls on Android and iOS devices.
