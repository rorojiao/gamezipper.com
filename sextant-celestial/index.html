How to Play Sextant Celestial

Sextant Celestial is a navigation instrument calibration puzzle. A sextant measures celestial angles through double reflection, and it has three calibration arcs — the Index arm (orange), the Vernier scale (cyan), and the Horizon glass (pink). Each arc carries a celestial marker (star). Your goal is to rotate the dials so that all three markers end up on the same radial line — a clean celestial sight that means the sextant is properly calibrated.

Each dial rotates all three arcs simultaneously by a fixed triple of vernier increments (shown as I±n V±n H±n, where n is in units of the current N). The challenge is finding the right combination of dials to press, since each press shifts all three arcs at once by different amounts. The arcs operate in modular arithmetic (mod N), where N ranges from 6 to 12 across levels.

Controls: Tap any dial to rotate all three arcs by its triple. Use ↶ Undo to reverse, ⟲ Reset to restart, ✓ Align? to check, and ▦ Levels for level select. On desktop: number keys (1–9) for dials, Z to undo, R to reset, Enter/Space to check.

Tips & Strategy

  • Look for co-alignment: When all three markers line up on the same radial line, a green circle appears around the sextant. Use this as your visual win indicator.
  • Analyze the triples: Each dial shifts I, V, and H by different amounts. The key insight is finding which dial combination makes the differences between the three arcs equal (since co-alignment means I=V=H mod N).
  • Stars reward efficiency: Three stars for matching the optimal move count, two stars within opt+2, one star otherwise. Undo and retry to find the shortest solution.
  • Later levels add dials and positions: Levels 1–5 have 2 dials (N=6), 6–10 have 3 dials (N=8), 11–15 have 3 dials (N=10), 16–20 have 3 dials (N=12), and 21–30 have 4–5 dials (N=12).
