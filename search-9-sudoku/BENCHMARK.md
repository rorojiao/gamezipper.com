# Search-9 Sudoku — Competitive Benchmark

## Game Overview
Search-9 Sudoku is a 9×9 Sudoku variant where clues **outside** the grid indicate the
exact **position** of the digit 9 in each row and column. A number to the **left** of a
row gives the 1-indexed **column** of the 9 in that row; a number **above** a column
gives the 1-indexed **row** of the 9 in that column. Standard Latin-square Sudoku rules
(each row, column and 3×3 box contains 1–9 exactly once) still apply.

All 18 outside clues (9 left + 9 top) are always visible, giving the solver an
immediate "treasure map" for the nine 9s before the rest of the grid is tackled.

## Level Design Notes

- **Grid size**: all 27 levels are classic **9×9** (3×3 boxes). Search-9 is inherently a
  9-based variant, so no 4×4/6×6 easing grids are used.
- **Given counts by tier** (fewer givens = harder):
  - Beginner: **30 givens**
  - Easy: **25 givens**
  - Medium: **22 givens**
  - Hard: **20 givens**
  - Expert: **18–19 givens**
  - Master: **17–19 givens**
- **Clue structure**: every level exposes all 18 position clues
  (`clues.L` = 9 left-row positions, `clues.T` = 9 top-column positions). These are
  derived directly from the solution, so a correctly-solved grid automatically
  satisfies every clue.
- **Uniqueness**: each puzzle was generated by a Latin-square digger and **verified to
  have exactly one solution** by an independent backtracking solver
  (`verify_independent.js`). No level requires guessing.
- **Solving arc**: the position clues let a solver place all nine 9s immediately, which
  cascades into the boxes/columns — making even sparse Master grids tractable with pure
  logic. The tier ladder ramps difficulty by reducing givens and increasing the
  reasoning depth between clue-driven placements.
