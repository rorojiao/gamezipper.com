# Competitive Benchmark: "Tens" Number Puzzle Games

> **Date:** May 29, 2026  
> **Purpose:** Competitive landscape analysis for a "Tens"-style number placement puzzle game  
> **Core Mechanic Reference:** Place number bubbles on a grid; adjacent numbers summing to 10 are cleared; strategic placement is key.

---

## Executive Summary

The "sum to 10" number puzzle niche is **crowded but fragmented**. No single dominant player owns the specific "place bubbles on grid, sum-10 clears" mechanic. The closest competitors span web games, mobile apps, and console titles. Key opportunities exist in combining the **placement strategy** of block-dropping games with the **sum-to-10 clearing** mechanic, which is under-served compared to simple pair-matching games.

## Competitor #1: TENS! (by Kwalee Ltd)

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Platforms** | iOS, Android (2017), Nintendo Switch and Steam (2020) |
| **Developer** | Kwalee Ltd (UK), console port by Sock Monkey Studios |
| **Total Downloads** | Part of Kwalee's 700M+ total (TENS! was #1 in "Dice" category in 67 countries) |
| **Price** | Free-to-play (mobile), Premium (Switch/Steam) |

### Core Mechanics
- **Grid:** 5x5 grid
- **Gameplay:** Drag numbered dice (1-9) onto the grid. When any row or column sums to exactly 10, the entire row/column clears and scores points.
- **Combines:** Block-dropping placement + Sudoku-style row/column sum logic
- **Obstacles:** Crate blocks and special tiles (bombs, boosters)
- **Special Tiles:** Bombs to clear areas, boosters for power plays

### Scoring System
- Points awarded for clearing rows/columns (each "TENS!" = cleared line)
- Combo system for chaining clears
- High score tracking with social leaderboards

### Level/Progression
- **Endless Mode:** Unlimited play, chase high scores
- **Adventure Mode:** 100+ curated puzzles, easy-to-hard difficulty ramp
- **Unlockables:** Dice skins earned through challenges
- **Console version:** Premium puzzle adventure with no IAP

### Visual Style
- Clean, colorful dice-themed aesthetic
- Bold number rendering on dice faces
- Smooth drag-and-drop animations
- Bright, family-friendly UI (hyper-casual style)

### Monetization
- **Mobile:** Free-to-play with ads + IAP (hints, boosters, no-ads)
- **Console/PC:** Premium paid title (no IAP)
- **Estimated DAU:** ~100K-300K (based on category ranking history and Kwalee's overall MAU profile)

### Critical Reception
- OpenCritic: 76/100 ("Strong"), 75% critic recommendation
- 38,000+ reviews on AppGrooves
- Nintendo Life review positive for accessibility

## Competitor #2: Numbers / "Number Match" (by Potato Jam, on Poki)

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Platforms** | Web (Poki.com), mobile browser |
| **Developer** | Potato Jam |
| **Price** | Free (browser game, ad-supported) |
| **URL** | https://poki.com/en/g/numbers |

### Core Mechanics
- **Grid:** Large grid of pre-filled number cells (not a placement game - a matching game)
- **Gameplay:** Tap pairs of identical numbers OR pairs summing to 10. Pairs must be adjacent (vertically/horizontally) or at the end of one line and beginning of the next. First and last numbers in the puzzle can also pair.
- **Key Difference:** This is a **matching/pairing** game, NOT a placement game. Numbers are pre-filled.

### Scoring System
- Points for each matched pair
- Bonus for clearing the board
- Level completion tracking

### Level/Progression
- Multiple procedurally generated boards
- Difficulty increases as boards get larger and numbers more varied
- No explicit level counter - endless puzzle format

### Visual Style
- Simple 2D tile/grid design
- Clean number display on colored blocks
- Minimalist web-game aesthetic (HTML5)
- Poki-branded wrapper UI

### Monetization
- Poki platform ads (pre-roll, mid-roll)
- No IAP
- **Estimated DAU:** ~50K-150K (Poki reaches 100M+ MAU; this is a mid-tier puzzle title)

## Competitor #3: Numberzilla (by Appsulove / AppCraft)

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Platforms** | iOS, Android |
| **Developer** | AppCraft LLC / Appsulove |
| **Price** | Free-to-play |
| **Reviews** | 248,000+ reviews on AppGrooves |

### Core Mechanics
- **Grid:** 2D grid of pre-filled numbers (rows of digits 1-9)
- **Gameplay:** Cross out pairs of same numbers (4-4, 9-9) or pairs summing to 10 (2-8, 3-7). Pairs must be adjacent (vertical/horizontal) or at row boundaries with only empty cells between them.
- **Key Difference:** Classic "Numberama / Take Ten / 10 Seeds" pen-and-paper game digitized. **Matching**, not placement.

### Three Game Modes
1. **Classic:** Endless, unlimited time and field. Add rows with + when stuck.
2. **Survival:** Limited field, Tetris-style block drops. Game over when board fills.
3. **Dynamic:** Complete a level then shuffle button multiplies points and advances to next level.

### Scoring System
- Points per pair cleared
- Time records and achievement badges
- Daily challenge code-cracking mini-game for bonus rewards

### Level/Progression
- Unlimited procedurally generated levels
- Daily challenges with special rewards
- Badge/achievement system with mascot characters
- Streak tracking

### Visual Style
- Cute mascot-driven (Numberzilla monster + food characters like Smart Croissant, Sassy Avocado)
- Colorful, playful, cartoony
- Animated character badges and rewards
- Designed to feel "zen" and relaxing

### Monetization
- **Ads:** Full-screen ads between rounds, rewarded ads for boosters
- **IAP:** Premium subscription (unlimited hints/swaps/bombs, no ads)
- **Boosters:** Hints, Bombs (clear 9 numbers), Swaps (reposition numbers)
- **Shop:** Buy boosters directly, "No ads" purchase
- **Estimated DAU:** ~200K-500K (based on 248K+ reviews and sustained chart presence)

| Attribute | Details |
|---|---|
| **Platforms** | Web (HTML5, multiple game portals) |
| **Developer** | Famobi |
| **Price** | Free (ad-supported) |

### Core Mechanics
- **Grid:** Filled grid of colorful numbered tiles (starting from 1)
- **Gameplay:** Double-tap to merge adjacent same-number tiles. Merged tile becomes next-higher number (e.g., two 3s become one 4). Goal: reach the number 10.
- **Key Difference:** This is a **merge/combine** game (like 2048), not a placement game or pair-matching game.

### Scoring System
- Score based on tiles merged and highest number achieved
- Higher merges = exponentially more points

### Level/Progression
- Single endless board
- Increasing difficulty as numbers climb
- No explicit levels

### Visual Style
- Colorful gradient tiles (each number has a distinct color)
- Smooth, satisfying merge animations
- Simple, clean HTML5 web-game aesthetic
- Mobile-responsive touch controls

### Monetization
- Portal ads (pre-roll, banners)
- No IAP
- **Estimated DAU:** ~20K-80K across all portals

## Key Competitive Insights

### 1. Placement + Sum-to-10 is Under-Served
Only **Kwalee's TENS!** combines *strategic placement* with *sum-to-10 clearing* in a row/column mechanic. All other competitors are either **pair-matching** (select two adjacent numbers that sum to 10) or **merge-up** games (combine same numbers). The "place a number bubble, adjacent sum-to-10 clears" mechanic is a genuine white space.

### 2. Pair-Matching Dominates the Sum-10 Niche
Numberzilla, Sum 10, and Poki Numbers all use the classic Numberama/Take Ten paper-game mechanic (tap adjacent pairs summing to 10). This is the dominant "sum to 10" game type. **Differentiation requires moving beyond pair-matching.**

### 3. Grid Size Sweet Spot: 5x5 to 8x8
- TENS! uses 5x5 (the industry reference)
- Merge 10 uses 10x10 (feels too large for tension)
- Most successful puzzle games (2048, Sudoku) use 4x4 to 9x9
- **Recommendation:** 5x5 for classic mode, 6x6 or 7x7 for extended play

### 4. Monetization Patterns
- **Mobile:** Free-to-play with ads + IAP (boosters, hints, ad removal) is the standard
- **Web:** Ad-supported (pre-roll + mid-roll), no IAP
- **Premium subscription** ($3-5/month) works for dedicated puzzlers (Numberzilla model)
- **Rewarded video ads** for boosters are universal
- **No paid-upfront mobile** games succeed in this niche

### 5. Visual Style Trends
- **Bright, flat, colorful** is the standard (think candy colors)
- **Cute mascots** boost engagement (Numberzilla's monsters)
- **Minimalist** design works for brain-training positioning (Sum 10)
- **Dice/bubble aesthetics** suit the number theme (TENS!, our target mechanic)
- **Satisfying animations** on clear/merge are critical for retention

### 6. Retention Mechanisms
- Daily challenges (Numberzilla, Sum 10) - strong for DAU
- Social leaderboards (TENS!) - drives competition
- Achievement/badge systems - long-term progression
- Streak tracking - habit formation
- Multiple game modes - breadth of engagement

### 7. Market Size
- Kwalee (parent of TENS!) has 700M+ total downloads; TENS! was #1 in Dice in 67 countries
- Numberzilla has 248K+ reviews suggesting 25M+ total downloads
- The "number puzzle" category on app stores is consistently in the top-20 puzzle sub-genre
- Combined DAU of identified competitors: **~500K-1.5M globally**

### 8. Platform Strategy
- **Mobile-first** is mandatory (iOS + Android)
- **Web/Poki** distribution for discoverability and virality
- **Console/PC** as a premium follow-up (TENS! proved this works)
- **No major competitor has a strong web + mobile unified experience**

## Gap Analysis: Opportunity for a New "Tens" Game

| Gap | Opportunity |
|---|---|
| **No bubble-aesthetic placement game** | Combine bubble visuals + grid placement + sum-to-10 clear |
| **Pair-matching fatigue** | Players bored of Numberama clones want fresh mechanics |
| **No strong web version of placement-style** | Only TENS! does placement; it's mobile/console only |
| **Weak social features** | Most competitors lack real-time leaderboards or competitive modes |
| **No procedural challenge mode** | Combine placement + daily challenge + difficulty scaling |
| **Underserved casual-to-midcore** | Most are either too casual (pair-match) or too niche (Sudoku) |
