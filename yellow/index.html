How to Play Yellow

Yellow is a color puzzle where each of the 30 levels uses a different trick to turn the entire screen yellow. There is no single rule — some levels ask you to tap a dot, drag a slider, tap four corners, spell a word (Y-E-L-L-O-W), count shapes, hold a button, solve a mini lights-out grid, navigate a maze, balance a scale, or catch an orbiting dot at the right moment. Read the hint (💡 button) if you're stuck; it describes the level's mechanic. Solve a level and the screen floods yellow, advancing you to the next. Progress is saved automatically.

Tips & Strategy

Approach each level fresh — the solution to level 5 won't help on level 6. Watch for subtle clues: a slightly off-color tile, a barely visible border, text that's also an instruction. Try every interaction: tap, drag, hold, swipe, and even multi-tap. Timing levels (moving bars, growing circles) reward patience — wait for the target window. Memory and sequence levels require you to watch first, then repeat. Use the hint button freely; it points you toward the mechanic without handing you the answer.
