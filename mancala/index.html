How to Play Mancala

Objective

Collect more stones in your store (the large pit on your right) than your opponent.

Setup

Each player has 6 small pits with 4 stones each, and one large store. Player 1 is on the bottom, Player 2 is on the top.

On Your Turn

  • Click one of your pits to pick up all stones from it.
  • Stones are distributed counter-clockwise, one per pit.
  • You skip your opponent's store but drop in your own.

Extra Turn

If your last stone lands in your store, you get another turn!

Capture

If your last stone lands in an empty pit on your side, you capture that stone plus all stones in the opposite pit. They all go to your store.

Game End

When a player has no stones left in their pits, the other player collects all remaining stones. The player with more stones in their store wins!

Mancala - Free Online Strategy Board Game

Play Mancala (Kalah) online for free — the classic African stone-counting strategy game that's been played for thousands of years. Challenge our AI opponent or play locally with a friend. With a built-in tutorial, it's perfect for fans of board games, strategy games, and classic two-player games. No download required.

How to Play Mancala

  • The board has 12 small pits (6 on each side) and 2 large stores (Mancalas) at the ends.
  • On your turn, pick up all stones from one of your 6 pits.
  • Sow them counterclockwise — drop one stone in each subsequent pit, including your Mancala.
  • Skip your opponent's Mancala when sowing.
  • If your last stone lands in your Mancala, you get a bonus turn.
  • If your last stone lands in an empty pit on your side, you capture it plus any stones in the opposite pit.
  • The game ends when one side has no stones — the player with the most stones wins.

Tips & Strategy

  • Extra turns are key — count ahead to land your last stone in your Mancala for a free move.
  • Keep your rightmost pits well-stocked early — they're closest to your Mancala and easier to score from.
  • Watch for capture opportunities — emptying a pit at the right time can steal a big haul from across the board.
  • Don't over-empty your side too early — you'll give your opponent the endgame advantage.
  • In the endgame, forcing your opponent to run out of stones first can swing the score dramatically.
  • Play the tutorial mode first to internalize the sowing and capturing rules.
