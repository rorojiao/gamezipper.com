Why Nuranaito?

Nuranaito (ぬらナイト, “paint with the knight's move”) is a Nikoli shading puzzle. White cells with numbers act as “knight’s-move detectors” — each shows how many black cells are exactly 2+1 squares away in an L-shape. The 2×2 rule and connectivity constraint make every puzzle a tight logical knot.

How to Play Nuranaito

Setup. The grid is N×N. Some cells contain a number, a ? mark, or are blank.

Goal. Blacken cells to satisfy all constraints simultaneously.

Rule 1 — Connectivity. All black cells must form ONE orthogonally connected area.

Rule 2 — No 2×2. No 2×2 block can be entirely black.

Rule 3 — White cells. Cells with numbers, “?” marks, or unfilled blanks that you choose to leave white must remain white.

Rule 4 — Knight-move count. A numbered cell tells you exactly how many black cells are reachable by a knight move from it. A knight moves in an L: 2 squares one way + 1 square perpendicular (8 possible directions).

Strategy. Start by deducing cells whose knight-move count is 0 — all 8 knight-targets must be white. Cells with high counts (4+) constrain many squares. The 2×2 rule and connectivity then narrow the rest.

• Shade mode: tap a blank cell to mark it black; tap again to clear.
• Erase mode: tap a black cell to clear it.
• Check: validates your current state. Errors are highlighted.
• Hint: reveals one correct black cell. You have 3 per level.
• Clear: removes all your shading.
