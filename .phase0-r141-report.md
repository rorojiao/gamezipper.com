### Rules (verified from cross-plus-a.com "Doppelblock" entry)
1. Square grid (N×N).
2. **Blacken exactly 2 cells in each row AND each column** (so 2N black cells total).
3. Fill remaining white cells with digits **1 to N-2** (each digit appears exactly once per row and once per column).
4. **Numbers outside the grid show the sum of the white digits between the two black cells** in the corresponding row or column.
5. (Implicit) Every puzzle must have a unique solution.

### Why this is ideal for Round 141
- **Pure constraint satisfaction**: Standard Latin-square-style with an extra black-cell constraint layer → easy to model & solve.
- **Clear generation strategy**: Place blacks → solve number constraints → verify uniqueness.
- **Mathematically clean**: N-2 digits × N rows means no duplicates per row/col; black cells break runs for sum clues.
- **Highly visual**: Colored clue numbers outside, digits in cells, two black squares per row/col create clean visual patterns.
- **Suitable for 30 levels**: 5x5 (3 digits) → 10x10 (8 digits), with constraint complexity scaling predictably.

### Differentiation from existing catalog
- Closest existing games: Sudoku (number placement), Hitori (shading), Kakuro (sum clues) — but NONE combine all three mechanics (shading + unique digits + sum-of-intervening).
- Conceptis/Skyscrapers/Suguru/Kakuro all use single-mechanic approaches.
- Doppelblock's "two blacks per row+col" creates a structural constraint missing from all current 680 entries.
