How to Play

Word Connections is a fun and challenging puzzle game where you need to find groups of four related words from a grid of sixteen words. Each puzzle contains four hidden groups, and your goal is to discover all of them.

The four groups in each puzzle range from easy to expert difficulty, indicated by color: Yellow is easiest, Green is medium, Blue is hard, and Purple is expert level.

You can make up to four mistakes before the game ends. Use the Reveal button to get a hint about one correct word (limited to three hints per puzzle).

Tips for Success

  • Look for common themes between words first. Sometimes the connection is obvious, like fruits or animals.
  • Other times it requires more creative thinking, like words that can follow or precede the same word.
  • Don't be afraid to guess, but be strategic. If you find one wrong combination, eliminate those words from your thinking and try a different grouping.
  • The hint system reveals one correct word in an unsolved group. Use it wisely when you're truly stuck.
  • Start with the easiest-looking words and work your way up to more challenging connections.
  • Pay attention to word patterns - compound words, rhymes, and similar structures often hint at connections.
  • Consider multiple meanings of words - a word might belong to different categories depending on its usage.
  • Shuffle the grid to get a fresh perspective on the words and potential connections.

Puzzle Difficulty Breakdown

  • Easy (Puzzles 1-12): Obvious categories like fruits, colors, animals, and everyday objects. Perfect for beginners learning the game mechanics.
  • Medium (Puzzles 13-30): Wordplay, double meanings, and less obvious connections. Requires more creative thinking and vocabulary knowledge.
  • Hard (Puzzles 31-42): Tricky connections, niche knowledge, and complex word relationships. Challenging even for experienced puzzle solvers.
  • Expert (Puzzles 43-50): Heavy wordplay, puns, multi-meaning words, and sophisticated puzzle patterns. The ultimate test for word puzzle masters.
