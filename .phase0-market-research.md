## 🏆 SELECTED CANDIDATE: Pulley Lift — Mechanical Advantage Puzzle

### Why Pulley Lift wins (22/25)
- **Search demand (4/5)**: "pulley puzzle", "mechanical advantage", "rope and pulley" — moderate but dedicated audience; educational physics games are a strong niche
- **Implementation (4/5)**: Grid-based routing puzzle with deterministic verification (mechanical advantage = count of rope segments supporting the load). BFS solvable.
- **Mobile (5/5)**: Tap-to-connect interaction, drag rope ends. Most mobile-friendly possible.
- **Depth (5/5)**: Fixed pulleys (1:1), movable pulleys (2:1), compound systems (4:1, 6:1), multiple loads, balanced systems, limited rope length
- **SEO (4/5)**: "pulley", "mechanical advantage", "rope pulley", "block and tackle" — all zero competition, strong educational keyword cluster

### Game: Pulley Lift — Rope & Pulley Logic Puzzle
- **Slug**: `pulley-lift`
- **Category**: puzzle (pulley + rope + mechanical advantage + physics + logic)
- **Core Mechanic**: Thread a rope through a system of pulleys (fixed and movable) to create the right mechanical advantage ratio. Lift the target weight to the top using the minimum number of rope segments.
- **Differentiation vs existing rope games**:
  | Existing | Overlap | Pulley Lift difference |
  |----------|---------|------------------------|
  | Cut the Rope (1 game) | rope | Cut rope — NOT route it through pulleys |
  | Rope Rescue (1 game) | rope | Tie/wrap rope around obstacles — NOT mechanical advantage |
  | Twisted Tangle (1 game) | rope | Untangle yarn — NOT lifting physics |
  | Balance Scale (1 game) | physics | Coin-weighing deduction — NOT pulley systems |

### Tier Plan (30 levels)
1. Tier 1 (1-5): Single fixed pulley — redirect rope, 1:1 ratio
2. Tier 2 (6-10): Single movable pulley — 2:1 mechanical advantage
3. Tier 3 (11-15): Compound systems (block and tackle) — 3:1, 4:1 ratios
4. Tier 4 (16-20): Multiple loads — lift two weights with shared rope
5. Tier 5 (21-25): Balanced systems — counterweight equilibrium
6. Tier 6 (26-30): Complex networks — limited rope, optimal routing

### Technical Approach
- Grid data model: cells contain pulley anchors (fixed/movable) or empty
- Rope routing: player draws rope path through pulley cells
- Mechanical advantage = number of rope segments supporting the movable pulley
- Win condition: mechanical advantage × player pull force ≥ load weight, and load reaches target height
- Solver: BFS through possible rope routings to verify unique solutions
