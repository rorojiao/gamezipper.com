How Brain Training Games Work

Your brain is like a muscle — the more you challenge it, the stronger it gets. Brain training games work by creating "desirable difficulties" — small challenges that push your cognitive abilities just beyond your comfort zone. This triggers neuroplasticity, the brain's ability to form new neural connections.

Research on Brain Training

Multiple studies from institutions like MIT and the University of Cambridge have shown that regular puzzle and memory game play can improve cognitive function in both children and adults. A 2023 study in the journal Nature Human Behaviour found that daily puzzle players showed measurable improvement in working memory and processing speed.

💡 Scientific Tip: For best results, play brain training games in 15-20 minute sessions rather than marathons. Short, focused sessions create more neural connections than prolonged, fatigued practice.

How to Build a Brain Training Routine

  • Morning: Start with a quick puzzle (5 minutes) to activate problem-solving circuits
  • Midday: A memory game during a break can reset mental fatigue
  • Evening: Word games before bed build vocabulary while being relaxing

What to Track

Keep a simple log of your scores over time. Improvement in brain games often comes in plateaus — you may not see progress for days, then suddenly jump forward. GameZipper's games all track your high score locally, so you can watch your improvement over weeks.

Combine Physical and Mental Exercise

Research consistently shows that combining aerobic exercise with cognitive training produces better brain health outcomes than either alone. A 20-minute walk combined with a 10-minute puzzle session is more effective than an hour of puzzles alone.
