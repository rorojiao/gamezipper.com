How to Play

  • Divide the entire grid into L-shaped trominoes (3 connected cells forming an L).
  • Each L-tromino must contain exactly one circle.
  • A black circle marks the corner (inner bend) of an L.
  • A white circle marks the tip (end) of an L.
  • L-trominoes cannot overlap and must cover every cell exactly once.
  • Click and drag across cells to draw region boundaries.

Tips & Tricks

  • Start with the circles: Each circle belongs to exactly one L. Work outward from circles to determine the L's orientation.
  • Check the corners: Black circles sit at the bend. The L extends in two perpendicular directions from it.
  • Mind the edges: Cells on the grid border have fewer possible L orientations — use them as anchors.
  • Look for forced cells: If a cell can only belong to one nearby circle's L, it must be part of that L.
  • Verify coverage: Every cell must be in exactly one L. If a cell is orphaned, revisit nearby regions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Sashigane?

Sashigane (指差し) is a Japanese logic puzzle where you divide a grid into L-shaped trominoes. Each L contains exactly one circle — black circles mark corners, white circles mark tips.

How do black and white circles differ?

Black circles sit at the inner corner (bend) of an L-tromino. White circles sit at the tip (endpoint). Both indicate exactly one L per circle.

Can I play Sashigane on my phone?

Yes, Sashigane is mobile-friendly. Tap and drag to draw L-shaped regions on your touchscreen.

Is Sashigane similar to other puzzles?

Sashigane shares DNA with other region-division puzzles like Shikaku and LITS, but the L-tromino constraint with circle markers makes it uniquely challenging.
