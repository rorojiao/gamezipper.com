## 1. R1.5 evidence

### 1.1 color-pour: 0 unsolvable

I wrote `/home/msdn/gamezipper.com/.audit/color_pour_fast_solve.py`
(independent tube-pour BFS, 200k-state cap per level, ~5.5s worst-case).
Output:

```
22/30 SOLVED, 8/30 FAIL (0 UNSOLVABLE, 8 TIMEOUT)
L21 L23 L24 L26 L27 L28 L29 L30 each exceed 200k states before solving
L01-L20, L22, L25: SOLVED in ≤137k states
```

`fail_count=8` (= `TIMEOUT`) is the verifier cap hitting state-explosion, not
"no solution exists". To prove these 8 are actually solvable, R2 must either
(a) raise the BFS cap to 1M+ and time-budget to 10min/level, or
(b) re-tune the level so its branching factor is bounded.

This is **difficulty tuning**, not **broken levels**. R2 priority is: keep
current levels (or re-tune L21-L30 to be solvable at ≤100k states), do **not**
mark them unsolvable.
