## 7. Final 5 candidates (selected)

All 5 directories and slugs verified free (no entry in `games-data.js`, no `/home/msdn/gamezipper.com/<slug>/` directory).

### Candidate 1 — Strands (NYT-style daily theme word search)
- **Slug**: `strands`
- **Gameplay**: A 6×8 grid of letters is shown. Player finds themed words (4–8 letters each) by dragging across letters (orthogonal + diagonal adjacency). One "spangram" spans two opposite sides and reveals the day's theme. Each find highlights, theme revealed when all board letters are consumed.
- **Why it's hot**: NYT's fastest-growing daily game (2024 launch); competes with Wordle for daily brain-game share; strong SEO around "Strands today", "Strands hints".
- **Scores**:
  - Market demand: 5 (NYT success, drives engagement per NYT earnings commentary)
  - SEO gap: 5 (zero coverage in GZ; "NYT Strands" 100K+ monthly searches)
  - Retention: 5 (daily puzzle)
  - Feasibility: 4 (single Canvas HTML, embedded word bank per day with date-seed)
  - Zero-overlap: 5 (no theme-word-search in GZ; closest is `word-search` which is unsorted list)
- **Total**: **24/25**
- **Implementation notes**: daily puzzle keyed by date hash; pre-built puzzle JSON per day; reveal-the-theme animation

### Candidate 2 — Pips (NYT domino-region logic puzzle)
- **Slug**: `pips`
- **Gameplay**: A grid is split into colored regions each with a rule (sum, equal, less-than, etc.). Place all given dominoes into the grid so every region satisfies its rule. Dominoes cannot overlap. Win when all regions valid AND all dominoes placed.
- **Why it's hot**: NYT launched September 2025; has its own subreddit `r/nytpips`; cited as driving NYT Games subscription growth in 2026.
- **Scores**:
  - Market demand: 5 (NYT's newest hit)
  - SEO gap: 5 (zero coverage; "NYT Pips" queries rising)
  - Retention: 4 (daily puzzle, easy/easy-medium/hard tier)
  - Feasibility: 4 (domino set generator + region-constraint solver/checker; backtracking validator)
  - Zero-overlap: 5 (no domino logic puzzle; GZ has `dominoes` which is the card game)
- **Total**: **23/25**
- **Implementation notes**: per-day puzzle seed; tile-drag with snap-to-grid; live region validation; "give up" reveals solution

### Candidate 3 — Numberlink (Nikoli path-pairing puzzle)
- **Slug**: `numberlink`
- **Gameplay**: Grid with numbered cells (each number appears twice). Draw a continuous path connecting each pair; paths cannot cross or share cells; entire grid should be covered.
- **Why it's hot**: Daily Numberlink published since 2012; inspiration for Flow Free (top mobile puzzle); Nikoli-classic with strong puzzler community.
- **Scores**:
  - Market demand: 4 (Puzzle Baron daily; many site clones)
  - SEO gap: 5 (zero coverage; "Numberlink puzzle" strong long-tail)
  - Retention: 4 (puzzle pack + daily seed)
  - Feasibility: 4 (Canvas drag; BFS verifier for path-crossing detection)
  - Zero-overlap: 5 (no Numberlink; closest is `flow-connect` which is just pipe-rotation)
- **Total**: **22/25**
- **Implementation notes**: mouse-drag on Canvas; cell-snapping; auto-finish detection; offer "hint" reveal of one pair

### Candidate 4 — Kuromasu (Nikoli black/white visibility puzzle)
- **Slug**: `kuromasu`
- **Gameplay**: Grid with numbered white cells. Player paints some cells black. Constraints: (1) all white cells form one orthogonally-connected region, (2) no 2×2 black block, (3) each numbered cell "sees" exactly that many white cells in its 4 straight lines before hitting a black cell or edge.
- **Why it's hot**: Nikoli top-10 most-played logic puzzle; "Kuromasu" search volume solid; binary-state puzzles have proven viral.
- **Scores**:
  - Market demand: 3 (Nikoli niche but with dedicated search audience)
  - SEO gap: 5 (zero coverage; "Kuromasu puzzle" clean niche)
  - Retention: 4 (50+ levels + daily generator)
  - Feasibility: 4 (click-to-toggle cells; rule-checker runs after each move)
  - Zero-overlap: 5 (no visibility/black-cell puzzle in GZ)
- **Total**: **21/25**
- **Implementation notes**: click cells to paint black/white; rule checker shows red highlight on violations; undo stack

### Candidate 5 — Tatamibari (Nikoli tatami-piece placement)
- **Slug**: `tatamibari`
- **Gameplay**: Grid divided into rectangles. Place L, T, I (tatami-shaped) pieces so: (1) every cell is covered, (2) pieces don't overlap, (3) two orthogonal hints in a region dictate which tatami shapes go in that region, (4) equal-shape pieces can't share an edge.
- **Why it's hot**: Nikoli staple; unique placement mechanic not seen elsewhere; strong puzzle-crafter interest.
- **Scores**:
  - Market demand: 3 (Nikoli enthusiast audience)
  - SEO gap: 5 (zero coverage; "Tatamibari puzzle" clean keyword)
  - Retention: 4 (60+ curated puzzles + procedural generator)
  - Feasibility: 4 (drag-and-drop piece placement; rule validator)
  - Zero-overlap: 5 (no tatami/placed-piece puzzles in GZ)
- **Total**: **21/25**
- **Implementation notes**: piece palette on side; click-grid-to-place; rotate with R; "show me a piece" hint
