# Tents (Tents and Trees) Puzzle — Competitive Benchmark

> **Puzzle Type**: Nikoli logic puzzle where players place tents next to trees on a grid.
> **Core Rules**: One tent per tree (horizontally/vertically adjacent), no tents touching (even diagonally), row/column tent counts must match given numbers.
> **Research Date**: June 2026

## Competitor Deep Dives

### 1. Tents and Trees Puzzles — Frozax Games

**The market leader.** The most feature-rich and commercially successful implementation. Originally mobile-only, expanded to all major platforms.

#### Platforms
- **Mobile**: iOS (App Store), Android (Google Play)
- **PC**: Steam, Epic Games Store
- **Console**: Nintendo Switch, PlayStation, Xbox (Play Anywhere, October 2024)
- **Price**: Free-to-play on mobile (with IAP); ~$9–$10 on console/PC

#### Level Counts & Progression
- **"Thousands of levels"** organized into level packs
- **Grid sizes**: 6×6, 7×7, 8×8, 9×9, 10×10, 12×12, 14×14, 16×16, 18×18, 20×20
- **Three difficulty modes per grid size**:
  - **Basic/Normal**: All row and column numbers visible; standard logic puzzles
  - **Hard**: More trees, fewer obvious starting points, requires deeper deduction
  - **No Grass / Missing Numbers**: Some row/column clue numbers are removed, forcing additional deduction. Significantly harder.
- Packs are named by size and variant (e.g., "No Grass 6×6 B", "12×18 A", "14×14 B")
- New packs added in updates (e.g., "New Pack 12×18 A, 14×14 B No Grass")
- Difficulty naturally scales with grid size — by 8×8, players have learned all techniques; larger grids are a matter of scale

#### Systems
| System | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Scoring** | Coin-based: **2 coins per completed level** (regardless of difficulty or size). Packs cost **40–250 coins**. Free coins available every few hours from main menu. Daily levels also earn coins. |
| **Hints** | **Unlimited free hints**: Highlights incorrect placements, marks completed rows/columns, suggests grass placement for empty cells, warns about tents touching each other. Icons appear when tents are placed adjacently (can be toggled on/off). |
| **Achievements** | iOS Game Center leaderboards and achievements. Console/PC achievement systems supported. |
| **Tutorials** | Implicit — the puzzle design itself acts as a tutorial. Early levels introduce techniques (e.g., marking rows with 0 tents as grass) without explicit instruction. |
| **Progress Saving** | Mid-level save supported. Can start a level, leave, and complete it later. Levels unlock in sets so you can skip and return to difficult ones. |
| **Daily Puzzles** | Yes — exclusive daily levels. More campaign levels completed = more daily levels unlocked. Daily levels also award coins. |
| **Multiplayer** | Up to **4-player local co-op** (console/PC). Each player has visual indicators showing their working area. |
| **Themes** | Multiple visual themes including dark mode. Themes unlockable through progression. |
| **Offline** | Full offline play supported. |

#### Visual Style
- **Nature-themed pastel aesthetic**: Soft greens, warm earth tones
- Trees appear as stylized tree icons; tents as small triangular tent shapes
- Grass cells are filled with green texture
- Clean grid with soft shadows and rounded corners
- Multiple unlockable color themes and dark mode
- **Portrait and landscape** modes supported on mobile

#### Music & Audio
- **Relaxing ambient nature-inspired soundtrack**
- Multiple music tracks to unlock
- Described as "beautifully pastel and meandering"
- Reviewers noted "pleasant but limited music selection"
- Fits the chill, no-time-pressure gameplay philosophy
- Audio feedback for tent placement, grass marking, and error states

#### Grid Design Philosophy
- Puzzles are **hand-crafted** (not procedurally generated)
- Excellent difficulty curve: introduces techniques through design, not text
- Each pack builds progressively on skills learned in previous packs
- Larger grids maintain engagement through scale rather than new mechanics
- "No Grass" mode provides a significant challenge variant for experienced players

### 2. Tents & Trails

**The premium free experience.** iOS-exclusive with a polished, modern design philosophy. Completely free with zero ads or paywalls.

#### Platforms
- **iOS only**: iPhone and iPad (requires iOS 18+)
- **Price**: Completely free — no ads, no paywalls, no IAP

#### Level Counts & Progression
- **Unlimited puzzles** (procedurally generated on-device)
- **Three game modes**:
  - **Daily Puzzles**: 3 new puzzles daily at midnight UTC (Easy, Medium, Hard). Same puzzles for all players worldwide.

| **Tutorials** | Implicit through Trail progression (starts easy, introduces concepts gradually). |

| **Daily Puzzles** | **Yes** — 3 per day (Easy, Medium, Hard) at midnight UTC. Build streak, compare solve times. |
| **Offline** | All puzzles generated on-device. Full offline play. iCloud sync only when online. |
| **Themes** | **6 hand-crafted color themes**: Classic Forest, Sakura Bloom, and 4 others. Each with unique icons and gradients. |

#### Visual Style

### Difficulty Progression Patterns

**Standard approach** (used by Frozax, Tents & Trails, brennerd):
1. **Easy**: Small grids (5×5–6×6), many 0-count rows/columns to fill with grass immediately, obvious tree-tent pairings
2. **Medium**: 7×7–10×10, fewer gimmes, requires chaining deductions across rows and columns
3. **Hard**: 10×10–14×14, no clear starting points, requires trying assumptions and backtracking
4. **Expert**: 14×14–20×20, dense tree placement, multiple valid-looking configurations that must be ruled out logically

**Frozax's "No Grass" variant**: Removes row/column clue numbers entirely from some rows/columns, adding a layer of meta-deduction (you must figure out which rows/columns could logically contain tents).

**Typical solve time ranges**:
- 6×6 Easy: 30 seconds – 2 minutes
- 8×8 Normal: 2–5 minutes
- 10×10 Hard: 5–10 minutes
- 15×15+ Hard: 10–30 minutes
- 20×20 Expert/No Grass: 20–60+ minutes

## Key Takeaways for New Entrants

### Must-Have Features (Table Stakes)
1. **Multiple grid sizes** (minimum 5×5 to 15×15; competitive up to 20×20)
2. **Progressive difficulty** from Easy to Expert with natural skill introduction
3. **Hint system** (at minimum: error highlighting; best: explanatory deductions)
4. **Progress saving** with mid-level resume
5. **Offline play** capability
6. **Daily puzzles** for retention
7. **Dark mode / theme support**

### Differentiation Opportunities
1. **Music/audio design** is almost completely untapped — only Frozax does it, and reviewers want more variety
2. **Scoring systems** are either non-existent or grindy — there's room for a satisfying, skill-based scoring formula
3. **"No Numbers" mode** is unique to Frozax — other variants could include partial visibility, time challenges, or combined rules
4. **Multiplayer** is nearly non-existent (only Frozax does local co-op) — online leaderboards or async multiplayer could differentiate
5. **Tutorial/onboarding** is mostly implicit — explicit interactive tutorials could improve retention
6. **Cross-platform sync** is rare — only Tents & Trails does iCloud sync
7. **Explanation-based hints** (brennerd's approach) are superior to simple reveals

### Pricing Lessons
- **Free with IAP** (Frozax): Dominant model but can frustrate users with slow coin economy
- **Completely free** (Tents & Trails): Best user reception but no revenue
- **One-time purchase** (Frozax console): $9–$10 price point is accepted
- **Free web** (all web competitors): No monetization, ad-supported possible

### Level Design Best Practices
1. Early puzzles should **teach through design** — not through text or tutorials
2. Each new technique should be introduced with puzzles that **require it** before moving on
3. Larger grids should scale difficulty through **density and complexity**, not new mechanics
4. Every puzzle must have **exactly one unique solution** solvable by **pure logic** (no guessing)
5. "No Grass/Missing Numbers" variants provide excellent late-game challenge without requiring new mechanics
