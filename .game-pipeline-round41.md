# Phase 0 Market Research — Top 5 Puzzle Game Candidates
**Date:** 2026-06-26
**Researcher:** dev-gamezipper subagent
**Sources:** Poki.com (Puzzle category, 250+ games), CoolmathGames (Puzzle category), CrazyGames.com (Puzzle category), Skorven, individual game pages, Wikipedia, GZ existing-games audit
**GZ current state:** 417 games live (334 puzzle). Last deployed: Cube Pop (#435, 2026-06-25), Color Lines (#436, 2026-06-25). Territory Draw (#434), Balance Scale (#433) shipped earlier in Round 38/39.
**Scoring rubric (each axis 1-5, total /25):**
1. **Market appeal** — downloads, ratings, brand recognition
2. **SEO gap** — search demand for the term vs GZ coverage
3. **Session engagement** — addictiveness, retention, "one more game" loop
4. **Feasibility** — buildable as a single-file HTML5 game in <30KB
5. **Zero-overlap** — 3-layer overlap gate (slug ≠ GZ; category ≠ GZ; core mechanic ≠ GZ)

## Rank 1 — Unpuzzle (Kek Games) — **23/25** ✅ RECOMMENDED

**Core mechanic (1 sentence):** Start with a fully-assembled jigsaw, then drag pieces away to remove them — you can only remove a piece if nothing is blocking it. Goal: empty the canvas. Brilliant inversion of the jigsaw puzzle.

**Why it wins:** The "reverse jigsaw" is a viral hook — players who *think* they know how jigsaws work are immediately surprised. Pure drag-and-release, no time pressure, no score, deeply satisfying tactile feel. Heavily replayable.

**Evidence:**
- 14,974 votes, 4.2⭐ on Poki (Poki game page verified)
- Maker Kek Games also published *UnpuzzleX* (Poki) and *Lines to Fill* — proven quality studio
- Series has been live on Poki since July 2021, updated Feb 2022
- 1000+ levels in mobile version of Unpuzzle (Brain Test–style progression)
- Featured in "Brain Games" and "Jigsaw Puzzle Games" on Poki

**3-layer overlap gate:**
- Layer 1 (slug): `unpuzzle` not on GZ ✅
- Layer 2 (category): reverse-jigsaw — no GZ counterpart ✅
- Layer 3 (mechanic): we have `jigsaw-puzzle` (drag-into-place) but **no** "drag-out-of-place" / "disassemble the picture" mechanic ✅
- **PASS**

**Scores:**
- Market 4 — Proven Poki presence, Kek Games' studio success
- SEO 4 — "unpuzzle" is unique, "reverse jigsaw" / "disassemble puzzle" all have search
- Session 5 — Brilliantly addictive, session-arc 1–3 min, 100+ levels potential
- Feasibility 5 — Canvas + per-piece bounding box + blocked-side check. ~600–800 lines JS
- Zero-overlap 5 — Genuinely distinct from all 417 GZ games

**Suggested slug:** `unpuzzle`
**Tier:** 1 (highest priority)
**Build complexity:** Low (well-understood drag-release with blocking logic)
**SEO opportunity:** "unpuzzle", "unpuzzle game", "reverse jigsaw", "disassemble jigsaw puzzle", "drag pieces out"

## Rank 2 — Hexxagon (a.k.a. Ataxx, Capcom 1990) — **21/25** ✅ RECOMMENDED

**Core mechanic (1 sentence):** Two-player strategy on a 7×7 hex board. On your turn, click an empty cell adjacent to one of your pieces to **spread** (clone) into it, or click a cell two-away with a clear cell in between to **jump** (move) into it. After every move, all enemy pieces adjacent to the new position flip to your color. Game ends when board is full or neither can move. Whoever owns more cells wins.

**Why it wins:** Hex grid + spreading/jumping captures is a well-balanced 2-player duel. The capture-by-proximity is a unique mechanic that creates tense territory fights. The hexagonal adjacency structure is visually distinctive. Single-player vs AI is just as good as 2P. Classic name ("Hexxagon" was the original DOS/Windows shareware name, still highly searched).

**Evidence:**
- 1990 Capcom/Leland arcade game
- One of the most popular DOS shareware games of the 90s (millions of installs)
- Still on Poki today ("Hexxagon" listing)
- Hexagon/Ataxx/Cloners all common names — strong existing search traffic
- Variant names: Ataxx, Cloners, Hexxagon, Hexagon game

**3-layer overlap gate:**
- Layer 1 (slug): `hexxagon` not on GZ ✅
- Layer 2 (category): 2-player strategy on hex board — no GZ game ✅
- Layer 3 (mechanic): our hex games are LOGIC (`hex-block`, `hex-haven`, `hex-minesweeper`, `hextris`, `hexa-sort`) — none are 2-player capture/strategy ✅
- **PASS**

**Scores:**
- Market 3 — Classic but not trending; niche but established audience
- SEO 4 — "hexxagon", "ataxx", "hexagon strategy game" all have steady search volume
- Session 4 — 2-player depth, AI modes, replayability, can play short matches
- Feasibility 5 — Hex grid + axial-coordinate math + BFS for adjacency + min-max AI. ~500–700 lines JS
- Zero-overlap 5 — No GZ 2-player hex strategy

**Suggested slug:** `hexxagon`
**Tier:** 1
**Build complexity:** Low (proven axial-coord hex math, can start with random-AI then add min-max)
**SEO opportunity:** "hexxagon", "ataxx", "hexagon strategy game", "hex board game", "cloners"

## Rank 3 — Apple Worm — **20/25** ✅ RECOMMENDED

**Core mechanic (1 sentence):** Snake-like worm that grows by eating apples. To pass through gaps or reach the portal, you must grow *just enough* — but if you grow too much, you get stuck and it's game over. 30 hand-crafted levels where the puzzle is fitting your body length through tight spaces.

**Why it wins:** Combines the universal "snake" instinct with level-based spatial planning. Each level is a tiny spatial-puzzle. The "grow or shrink" tension is a clean constraint that creates real decisions. CoolmathGames classic.

**Evidence:**
- On Poki (Poki game page verified, 4.4⭐)
- 30+ challenging levels in original
- Listed on CoolmathGames (top featured puzzle)
- Long history as Y8/Flash classic ("Apple Worm" by Oleg Skrypnyk, 2010s)
- Search terms: "apple worm", "apple worm game", "snake puzzle"

**3-layer overlap gate:**
- Layer 1 (slug): `apple-worm` not on GZ ✅
- Layer 2 (category): level-based spatial puzzle — partially overlaps with `snake` (classic endless) and `grow-worm` (generic) but the **level-based, fit-through-gaps** framing is a different sub-genre ✅
- Layer 3 (mechanic): our `snake` and `grow-worm` are endless/arcade; Apple Worm is level-based spatial-fitting ✅
- **PASS** (with note: avoid building it as endless mode — must be level-based to differentiate)

**Scores:**
- Market 3 — Coolmath/Poki classic, 10+ year legacy
- SEO 4 — "apple worm" + "apple worm game" have steady search
- Session 4 — 30+ level design + 3-star scoring = solid retention
- Feasibility 5 — Canvas + head/body segment list + apple pickup + collision. ~500 lines JS
- Zero-overlap 4 — Only mild mechanic overlap with `grow-worm`/`snake`, fully distinct as level-puzzle

**Suggested slug:** `apple-worm`
**Tier:** 1
**Build complexity:** Low
**SEO opportunity:** "apple worm", "apple worm game", "snake grow game", "worm fit puzzle"

## Rank 4 — Loop Maze (Skorven, 2024) — **19/25** ✅ RECOMMENDED (stretch)

**Core mechanic (1 sentence):** Navigate grid mazes where some doors only stay open while a pressure plate is held down — and you can't be in two places at once. The twist: press SPACE to record your movement as a "loop" / echo, which then replays your exact path. While your echo holds the plate, you take a different route. 5 worlds with escalating mechanics (lasers, teleporters, conveyors, up to 5 simultaneous echoes).

**Why it wins:** Time-loop puzzles are inherently clever, and the Braid/Baba-Is-You lineage gives GZ a "thinking-person" puzzle that complements our puzzle mix. Echo mechanics train spatial reasoning, planning, and sequential logic — far deeper than typical browser puzzle. The "wow" of seeing your past self trigger a switch is a viral moment.

**Evidence:**
- Skorven (independent browser-game studio) flagship title
- 75 hand-crafted levels across 5 themed worlds (verified via Skorven site scrape)
- Unique mechanics: Pressure Plates, Switches, Lasers, Teleporters, Conveyors
- "World 5: Paradox" requires 5 simultaneous echoes — extreme planning depth
- Featured on indie game directories; referenced in puzzle-game roundups
- No equivalent on Poki/CrazyGames (Skorven only)

**3-layer overlap gate:**
- Layer 1 (slug): `loop-maze` not on GZ ✅
- Layer 2 (category): time-loop action-puzzle — no GZ game ✅
- Layer 3 (mechanic): echo recording, replay-as-ghost, multi-echo coordination — zero overlap with any GZ game ✅
- **PASS** (caveat: high implementation complexity)

**Scores:**
- Market 3 — Niche-but-cult indie title; smaller audience than mainstream puzzle
- SEO 3 — "loop maze" + "echo puzzle" have modest search volume
- Session 5 — Highly addictive due to escalating challenge + "aha!" moments
- Feasibility 3 — Complex: grid maze + A* pathing + echo-replay timing + multi-mechanic state. ~1500–2000 lines JS
- Zero-overlap 5 — No comparable mechanic in GZ

**Suggested slug:** `loop-maze`
**Tier:** 1 (high-value but higher risk; recommend 2nd or 3rd in build queue after Unpuzzle ships)
**Build complexity:** **HIGH** (3 of 5 worlds MVP for ~30 levels is feasible; full 75 levels is multi-sprint)
**SEO opportunity:** "loop maze", "echo puzzle", "time loop puzzle", "ghost maze", "past self puzzle"

## Rank 5 — Mowing Mazes (Protostar) — **18/25** ✅ RECOMMENDED (volume play)

**Core mechanic (1 sentence):** Drive a lawnmower through garden mazes, cutting all the grass in intricate patterns. Meet quirky NPCs, complete mowing "contracts," and ultimately try to escape the Evermaze. 30+ levels with growing difficulty.

**Why it wins:** Combines the universally-loved "maze" trope with a fresh thematic wrapper (lawn care, garden adventure). The "mow the lawn" verb is satisfying — clear visual progress, easy to understand, low cognitive load. Cute cast of characters adds charm. Great casual session game, broad audience (kids, casual players, office breaks).

**Evidence:**
- On Poki (32.8K likes, 5.7K dislikes, 4.4⭐) — high engagement metrics
- Made by Protostar (first Poki title)
- Released April 2026 — recent, active player base
- Search terms: "mowing mazes", "lawn mower game", "maze game online"
- Appears in "Maze Games", "Tractor Games", "Green Game Jam" categories on Poki

**3-layer overlap gate:**
- Layer 1 (slug): `mowing-mazes` not on GZ ✅
- Layer 2 (category): garden/maze/simulation hybrid — no GZ game ✅
- Layer 3 (mechanic): mow-grass progression + garden theme — no overlap with `maze-runner` (endless runner), `maze-paint` (paint the path), or `country-road` (driving game) ✅
- **PASS** (with note: this is more simulation/casual than pure puzzle; build it with clear level objectives to keep it puzzle-y)

**Scores:**
- Market 3 — Strong Poki presence (32.8K), new (Apr 2026), broad casual audience
- SEO 3 — "mowing mazes" has modest search; "maze game" is huge but competitive
- Session 3 — Story/exploration pace; not "one more game" loop
- Feasibility 4 — Top-down grid + character movement + grass-tile fill + NPC dialog. ~800–1000 lines JS
- Zero-overlap 5 — Garden/mowing theme is fully new

**Suggested slug:** `mowing-mazes`
**Tier:** 2 (volume play; not viral-hook material, but solid evergreen)
**Build complexity:** Medium (NPC dialog, level design, sprite assets add effort)
**SEO opportunity:** "mowing mazes", "maze game online", "lawn mower game", "garden maze puzzle"
